Water Provenance

Where the pearl
begins.

Every pearl carries the chemical memory of the water in which it formed. This is not poetry — it is biochemistry. We source from three distinct bodies of water, each yielding a flavour signature as distinct as a vintage.

I. The Caspian Basin
Our founding waters. Cold, mineral-dense, with ancient saline stratification. Produces our flagship Oscietra and the Blanc de Blancs: complex, deep, and austere.
II. The Adriatic
Warmer, with higher calcium content from limestone seabed. Imparts a silken, nutty sweetness to our Dorée expression — the most approachable pearl in the collection.
III. The Amur River
Fed by glacial snowmelt across Siberia. The coldest origin in our portfolio. The Siberian Impérial reflects its source: clean, mineral, uncompromising.

The Lineage

A century of
not hurrying.

1921
The Foundation

Édouard Leclair establishes the Maison on the shores of the Caspian with a single principle: no harvest before the pearl is ready. The founding ledger contains one entry for the first season: "Waited."

1954
The Adriatic Accord

Third-generation Maison masters travel to the Adriatic coastline. A new strain of oscietra — with a distinctly silken character — is identified and cultivated. The Dorée expression is conceived.

1978
The White Pearl

A chance mutation in the Caspian stock produces an albino clutch with extraordinary flavour depth. The Blanc de Blancs programme begins. The first commercial release will not come for sixteen years.

2008
The Amur Expedition

Maison researchers travel to the Amur River basin in Siberia. The cold mineral character of the water is unlike anything in the portfolio. The Siberian Impérial programme is established.

2024
The Réserve Privée

The Maison opens a bespoke commission programme for the first time in its history. A maximum of twelve private clients per year. Each tin bears their name. The concierge answers personally.
