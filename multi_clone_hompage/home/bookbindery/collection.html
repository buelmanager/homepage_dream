Commission Types

What We Bind

Every commission at Volumen Atelier is unique. The six categories below represent the primary domains of our practice — but we consider any project where permanence is the objective and craft is the only standard that matters.

Estate Library
Full Leather Sets

Complete estate libraries in full calf, goatskin, or vellum — uniform across all volumes, with gold-tooled spines and matching decorative boards.

Conservation
Restoration & Rebinding

Original materials preserved wherever possible. Structure rebuilt using period-appropriate techniques. Used by museums, private collectors, and institutions.

Fine Binding
Bespoke Single Volumes

One volume, one binder, one vision. Fine binding commissions are the purest expression of the craft — each a singular object of permanent beauty.

Gold Tooling
Hand-Gilded Titles & Decoration

23.5 karat gold leaf applied with antique brass finishing tools. Each impression is deliberate, final, and unrepeatable — the binder's handwriting in gold.

Institutional
Museum & Archive Commissions

Purpose-built for longevity in controlled environments. Acid-free materials, archival adhesives, and conservation-grade structures for permanent collections.

Presentation
Solander Boxes & Clamshells

Cloth-lined storage boxes for rare books, manuscripts, and fragile materials. Built to the same standards as our bound volumes.

Our Materials

Only What Lasts
Five Centuries

We source exclusively from suppliers with documented provenance. Harmatan goatskin from England. Lyon moire silk from the Croix-Rousse district. Fabriano cotton rag paper, made without interruption since 1264.

Every material is selected for its archival properties first, its beauty second. We have never and will never substitute an inferior material for a superior one to meet a deadline or a budget.
