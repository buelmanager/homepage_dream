The Méndez-Castillo Legacy

Eighty-Six Years of Ceremony

The Founder

Alejandro Mendez-Castillo

Alejandro Méndez-Castillo arrived in Geneva in 1937 with a torcedor's chaveta and an absolute conviction that the finest cigars in the world were not yet being made. He established Casa Obscura the following year in a single room above a watchmaker's workshop on the Rue de Rive.

What distinguished Alejandro was not his skill — though that was considerable — but his refusal to compromise on time. He turned away patrons who wanted their cigars within a year. He destroyed blends that were technically correct but that he felt were "not yet speaking."

Four generations later, the maison operates from the same address, with the same philosophy.

The Foundation

Four Pillars

I
Silence

The rolling room operates in absolute quiet. Focus is measured in grams of filler.

II
Time

Minimum four years fermentation. No blend released before the Maître judges it complete.

III
Singularity

One torcedor per patron. The relationship is personal and cultivated over years.

IV
Restraint

We accept 12 new patrons per year. No retail. We make what we can make with integrity.
