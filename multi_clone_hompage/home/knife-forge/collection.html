Commission Types

Twelve Blades a Year

Every Ignis Lama blade begins with a commission brief. The six types below represent the range of work Marcus Volker currently accepts — each with different steel requirements, geometry, and intended use. No two blades are identical.

Kitchen
Chef's Knife
W2 / 1.2419 · 62–64 HRC

The commission that tests every dimension of the maker's skill. A chef's knife is used daily, hard. It must hold an edge, survive a professional kitchen, and feel inevitable in the hand.

Hunting
Field Knife
CPM-3V · 60–62 HRC

Built for extended outdoor use. CPM-3V for toughness over maximum hardness — a knife that will not chip in cold, will not rust in wet, and can be resharpened in the field.

Collector
Damascus Art Knife
Mosaic Damascus · Custom

The most time-intensive work Marcus accepts. A damascus art knife may involve two hundred layers of steel, forty hours of patterning, and a handle in fossil walrus or stabilised wood.

Kitchen
Nakiri
Blue #2 / Shirogami · 63 HRC

A single-bevel vegetable knife. Marcus makes six to eight per year, all in Japanese steel. The geometry takes longer to grind than any other profile he makes — and the edge, sharpened correctly, will last a year.

Outdoor
Bushcraft Knife
O1 / 90MnCrV8 · 60 HRC

Scandinavian grind. Full tang. Simple, absolute geometry designed for batoning, fire-starting, camp tasks. The knife that proves you do not need more than four inches and a convex edge.

Gift
Presentation Blade
Stainless Damascus · Custom

Commissioned for a specific person or occasion. Marcus takes these seriously: a presentation blade should be worth displaying and worth using. He refuses to make ceremonial blades that cannot hold an edge.

Materials

Steel First,
Everything Else Second

Marcus sources steel from four suppliers — two German, one Swedish, one Japanese. Every batch is mill-tested before it enters the forge. He has returned three batches in thirty years.

Handle materials are sourced with equivalent care: stabilised wood from a single supplier in Oregon, horn from a tannery in Bavaria, fossil materials from registered archaeological dealers only.
