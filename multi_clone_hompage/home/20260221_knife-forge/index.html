The Atelier

Where Fire Meets Precision

Four generations of bladesmithing knowledge live in our workshop. The forge has been continuously operating since 1887, its flames passed from grandfather to grandson, each adding their own chapter to the craft.

We work with traditional Japanese and European techniques, selecting only the finest steels—Aogami, AUS-10, and rare Damascus patterns forged in our own shop.

— The Marchetti Family

The Process

01 / SELECTION

Steel Selection

Raw steel is hand-selected for grain structure and carbon content. Only steels meeting our exacting standards proceed to the forge.

02 / FORGING

Hot Forging

At 1,200°C, the steel is shaped on the anvil. Each hammer blow is deliberate, building the blade's fundamental geometry and density.

03 / HARDENING

Heat Treatment

The critical stage. Temperatures controlled to ±5°C determine the final hardness. We quench in oil or water depending on steel composition.

04 / GRINDING

Edge Grinding

Hours of grinding on wet wheels create the bevels. The geometry is calculated for its specific purpose—push cutting, slicing, or delicate work.

05 / FINISHING

Hand Finishing

Final polish by hand. Handles are fitted and pinned. Each blade is test-cut before leaving the forge. The maker's mark is hammered in.
