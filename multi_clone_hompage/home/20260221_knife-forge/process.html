The Process

Each IGNIS LAMA blade undergoes five essential stages. No shortcuts. No automation. Only the maker's hands, trained over decades, guiding steel through its transformation.

01 / SELECTION

Steel Selection

The journey begins with raw steel. We select only Japanese AUS-10, Aogami (Blue Paper), and White #2 steels—each chosen for specific characteristics like edge retention, toughness, and ease of sharpening.

Carbon Content: 1.0-1.2% | Hardness: 60-64 HRC
02 / FORGING

Hot Forging

At precisely 1,200°C, the steel becomes malleable. Using traditional Japanese and European techniques, we hammer the blade into its initial form. Each strike is deliberate—the density and geometry are set here.

Temperature: 1,100-1,250°C | Hammer Weight: 2-4kg
03 / HARDENING

Heat Treatment

The most critical stage. We normalize the steel, then heat to critical temperature and quench—either in oil or water depending on the steel type. This determines the final hardness and edge characteristics.

Quench: Oil or Water | Tempering: 150-200°C
04 / GRINDING

Edge Grinding

Hours on wet grinding wheels create the bevels. The geometry is calculated based on the blade's intended use—thinner for slicing, thicker for robustness. We grind to 15°-20° inclusive angle for optimal sharpness.

Bevel Angle: 15-20° inclusive | Grit: 400-6000
05 / FINISHING

Hand Finishing

Final polish by hand using progressively finer compounds. Handles are fitted and pinned with stainless steel. The maker's mark is struck into the blade. Each knife is test-cut before leaving the forge.

Final Polish: 3-8µm | Test Cut: Tomato & Paper

Materials

AUS-10

Stainless Steel

Japanese stainless steel with excellent edge retention and corrosion resistance. Ideal for kitchen knives. Hardness: 59-61 HRC.

Aogami (Blue #2)

Carbon Steel

The "Blue Paper" steel. Premium Japanese carbon steel with exceptional sharpness and ease of sharpening. Requires maintenance.

White #2

Carbon Steel

High-carbon steel known for pure white steel purity. Takes incredibly fine edge. The choice for traditional Japanese knives.

Damascus

Pattern Steel

Multiple layers of different steels forge-welded together. Each pattern is unique. Functional beauty meeting traditional aesthetics.
