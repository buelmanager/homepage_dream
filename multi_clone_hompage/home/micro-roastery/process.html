The Process

// our method

"We treat every roast as a scientific hypothesis. The only valid test of a hypothesis is the evidence — and the evidence is in the cup."

The MERIDIAN process is built around a single commitment: every decision must be traceable, reproducible, and improvable. We do not roast by feel. We roast by data informed by accumulated experience — and when the data and the instinct disagree, we investigate rather than assume.

// five stages

Farm to
Extraction

// 01 — Origin Visit

Annual Producer Travel

Every producer we work with receives an annual visit during or shortly after harvest. We cup alongside them, share our previous year's roast data, and discuss what changed — and why. These conversations inform the next year's profile.

// 02 — Green Assessment

Arrival Cupping & Analysis

New-season lots are cupped blind against the SCA protocol by three tasters independently. Scores are averaged; disagreements of more than 2 points trigger a fourth tasting. No lot below 88 proceeds.

// 03 — Profile Development

Roast Curve Mapping

A minimum of 12 sample roasts across different charge temperatures, rates of rise, and development time ratios. Each is cupped at 8 minutes and 24 hours. The winning profile is locked and logged to our Analytics platform.

// 04 — Production

Precision Batch Execution

Production roasts must match the approved profile within 0.5°C at each logged control point. Any deviation outside tolerance is flagged, investigated, and the batch cupped again before release approval.

// 05 — Rest & Dispatch

7-Day Rest, Then Ship

All coffee rests for a minimum of 7 days at 18°C before packaging. This is not optional. CO₂ release during this window is the final stage of the roast — interrupting it produces a flatter, less expressive cup.

// equipment.md

The Tools of
Precision

Loring Smart Roaster S15

Equipment — Copenhagen Roastery

Three modified units with proprietary temperature sensors providing 0.5°C resolution. Loring's sealed roasting chamber eliminates smoke re-circulation for a cleaner cup character.

Roast Analytics Platform

Software — In-house Development

12,000 data points per session. Identifies anomalies in real-time and surfaces pattern divergences across historical batches. Built over three years and updated continuously.

Cropster Integration

Roast Log — External

All production roasts are also logged to Cropster for redundancy and external verification. Clients can request access to their lot's specific roast log on request.

SCA Cupping Protocol

Evaluation Standard

We use SCA standard cupping parameters for all green assessments. Roasted coffee is evaluated at 8 minutes and 24 hours post-roast using calibrated water at 93°C.
