Material Philosophy

The sap is everything

Urushi is the only lacquer we use. It is toxic to apply, demanding to store, sensitive to humidity and temperature, and irreplaceable in the warmth, depth, and durability it produces. It is also alive — a biological polymer that continues to crosslink for centuries after application.

漆 — Urushi Sap
Iwate Prefecture, Japan

We source only domestic Japanese urushi from tapped trees in Iwate Prefecture — the purest available. Chinese urushi, while less expensive, has different flow characteristics we cannot control to our standard.

木 — Wood Substrate
Selected Japanese hardwoods

The choice of ground material determines how the urushi behaves over centuries. We use cypress, zelkova, paulownia, and cherry — each selected for grain direction and density relative to the intended use of the object.

金 — Gold Powder
Kanazawa, Ishikawa Prefecture

Our maki-e gold powder comes from a single supplier in Kanazawa — the city that produces 99% of Japan's gold leaf and powder, and has done so for 400 years.
