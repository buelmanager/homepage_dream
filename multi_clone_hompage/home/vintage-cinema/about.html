Founded 1924

The Origin Story

In the winter of 1924, Édouard Lumière-Beaumont converted a derelict Belle Époque theatre on the Rive Gauche into what he called a "sanctuary of the projected image." His ambition was singular: to give cinema the reverence it deserved.

Where other exhibitors chased novelty, Lumière Noire cultivated depth. Édouard refused to show a film unless he had curated a complete contextual programme — letters, prints, period music — to surround it. The practice was considered eccentric. Within a decade, it was considered essential.

One hundred years later, the methods remain unchanged. Only the films grow older and more precious.

What We Believe

The Core Values

I
Authenticity Over Restoration

We screen films in their original format. Grain, scratches, and time are part of the work.

II
Context Is Everything

No film exists in isolation. Every screening is accompanied by its historical and artistic context.

III
Smallness as Luxury

We limit every screening to 24 guests. Exclusivity is not vanity — it is the condition of intimacy.

IV
Silence as Hospitality

We do not speak of what is shown. The experience is between the guest and the film.
