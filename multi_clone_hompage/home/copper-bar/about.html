About

The Premise

"Oxidation is not decay. It is the surface becoming honest about what it has been through."

Verdigris was opened in the belief that a cocktail bar can be a material practice. Not a lounge, not a service environment, but a working space where chemistry is the medium and copper is the language. Every drink made here is designed around the properties of copper — its reactivity, its warmth, its capacity to change. The patina on our bar top has been developing since we opened. We have never polished it.

The Founders

Elena Marsh
& Dario
Ferretti

Elena Marsh studied chemistry at Columbia before spending three years working in a craft distillery in Kentucky. Dario Ferretti trained as a metalworker in Turin before emigrating to New York, where he spent a decade as a bar consultant for hotel properties across the city. Their collaboration began over a disagreement about whether gin or copper was the more interesting subject.

The answer, they decided, was that the question was wrong. Copper is not a backdrop to gin. Copper is an ingredient. The still, the vessel, the surface of the bar — these are not furniture. They are participants in the drink's development, and a cocktail programme that ignores them is ignoring half its palette.

Verdigris opened in the Meatpacking District in 2018. The bar top was fabricated by Dario from fourteen-gauge sheet copper, and has been developing its patina on site ever since. The cocktail programme is designed by Elena and reviewed quarterly. No signature is permanent.

Principles

How Verdigris works

01

Chemistry as Craft

Every drink on the menu is designed around a chemical principle — pH, oxidation, reduction, enzymatic reaction. This is not for show. It produces results that cannot be achieved through standard bar practice, and it requires both technical knowledge and culinary sensibility in the same person.

02

Copper Through Every Stage

From the still that produces our house spirits to the cups in which certain drinks are served, copper is present as a functional material throughout. We use copper not for its aesthetic associations but because its thermal properties and surface reactivity genuinely affect the taste of what passes through it.

03

The Menu Expires

Verdigris's cocktail menu is reviewed and substantially rebuilt every quarter. No drink is permanent. This is not a marketing decision. It is a commitment to the same intellectual rigour in seasonal practice that any serious kitchen applies: the best drink for this month is not the same as the best drink for last month.

Timeline

Since we opened

2018

Opening

Verdigris opens at 47 Meatpacking District with a twelve-cocktail menu and a staff of four. The copper bar top — fabricated by Dario from sheet copper acquired in Turin — is installed the week before opening. Its patina begins that first night.

2019

The Still Arrives

A 40-litre Portuguese alembic still is installed in the back lab, enabling in-house spirit production for the first time. The first Verdigris house distillate — a copper-pot gin using botanicals selected for their copper-reactive compounds — is served exclusively at the bar.

2021

The Atelier Programme

The Atelier — a private tasting and workshop space seating up to eight — is opened for invited guests and members. The programme offers structured tasting sessions exploring the chemistry of specific ingredients: acids, tannins, copper compounds, volatile esters. Not cocktail masterclasses. Chemistry demonstrations with extraordinary drinks.

2024

Sixth Year

Verdigris enters its sixth year. The bar top patina has reached what Elena describes as "honest maturity." The cocktail menu has turned over twenty-four times. The still has produced eleven distinct house distillates. Reservations continue to be required; we have not changed our hours since opening.

Verdigris

Forged in Copper. Aged in Silence. Every cocktail is a material process; chemistry is craft.
