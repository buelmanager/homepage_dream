Extraction Protocol

The Method

The Single Variable

Temperature Is Not a Preference. It Is a Specification.

Most cold brew operations use "cold" loosely — ambient temperature, refrigerator approximations, or room temperature with ice. CRYO LAB operates with a controlled extraction chamber maintained at 2°C ± 0.5°C throughout the full 22-hour cycle. This is not aesthetics. It is the only way to achieve consistent batch-to-batch replication across six protocols.

Five Stages

From Origin to Bottle

01

Origin Selection

Dr. Nakamura visits each source farm at minimum once per growing season. Beans are assessed at origin: cherry ripeness, soil composition, fermentation control, and altitude. No broker relationships. Every origin is a direct partnership with documentation going back to the first delivery.

Annual farm visits · 12 origins max
02

Roast Calibration

Each origin receives a roast profile developed specifically for cold extraction — typically lighter than conventional roast points to preserve volatile acids and aromatic compounds that cold temperature would otherwise suppress. Roasting is performed in-house on a 12kg drum roaster to a precision of ±2°C development temperature.

In-house roasting · Protocol-specific profiles
03

Grind Precision

Grind consistency is the most critical variable after temperature. A coarser grind reduces surface area and extends extraction time; a finer grind risks over-extraction even at 2°C. CRYO LAB uses a flat-burr commercial grinder calibrated to ±3 microns. Grind checks are performed every four batches.

±3 micron tolerance · Flat-burr calibration
04

Controlled Immersion

Grounds are submerged in filtered water at 2°C within a sealed stainless chamber. The chamber is loaded into the extraction unit at precisely 06:00 each production day. Temperature is logged every 15 minutes. Any deviation exceeding 0.5°C triggers a batch hold. We have held — and discarded — three batches since 2020.

22-hour cycle · 15-minute temperature logging
05

Filtration and Bottling

At hour 22, extraction is interrupted and the liquid passes through a three-stage filtration system: coarse screen, fine screen, and a final paper filtration stage. Bottles are filled and sealed within 90 minutes of filtration to prevent oxidation. Each bottle receives a batch number, protocol designation, and extraction timestamp. Shelf life is 14 days sealed, 5 days opened.

3-stage filtration · 90-minute bottling window
The Instruments

Scientific Instruments. Not Coffee Equipment.

The CRYO LAB extraction facility in Bermondsey was designed as a food-science laboratory, not a coffee shop. Every instrument was specified for precision rather than aesthetics.

We measure what most coffee operations leave to intuition. Every variable that can be controlled is controlled. Every batch is documented. The result is replicable.

  • Stainless steel insulated extraction vessels (200L capacity)
  • Programmable PID temperature controller with alarm relay
  • 12kg flat-burr commercial grinder, calibrated quarterly
  • 12kg drum roaster with data-logging thermocouple
  • Three-stage filtration array (coarse / fine / paper)
  • Nitrogen-flush bottling station for oxidation prevention
  • Real-time temperature monitoring with cloud logging
