Expressions

The Harvests

Each Silvardin harvest is a singular event — named by year, distinguished by season, and irrepeatable. Once the allocation is exhausted, that expression exists only in the memories of those who tasted it.

Harvest 2024 · 312 Bottles · 44% ABV
Autumn Expression

The finest juniper crop in recorded history. Early frost followed by a long warm September. Resinous, bright, and uncompromising at the nose. Still available on the harvest list.

Harvest 2023 · 312 Bottles · 44% ABV · SOLD OUT
The Rain Year

A wet summer delayed the juniper harvest by two weeks. The elderflower, however, was extraordinary — picked at dawn before the dew lifted, in volumes twice the normal yield.

Harvest 2022 · 312 Bottles · 44% ABV · SOLD OUT
The Dry Summer

An unusually dry July concentrated the juniper oil to exceptional density. The 2022 expression is the most juniper-forward gin Silvardin has ever produced. Held in high regard by collectors.

Harvest 2021 · 312 Bottles · 44% ABV · SOLD OUT
Cardamom Dominant

The year the Kerala cardamom was particularly potent. An unusual batch — the finish lasts eleven minutes. Described by one taster as "a forest after a spice fire."

Harvest 2020 · 312 Bottles · 44% ABV · SOLD OUT
The Lockdown Vintage

Made in silence, during a year of extraordinary stillness. Ailsa foraged alone. The coriander seed, sourced from Bulgaria, arrived late — adding an accidental extra week of maceration. The result was different. Better.

Harvest 2019 · 312 Bottles · 44% ABV · SOLD OUT
The Original Formula

The harvest that established Silvardin's reputation. Unchanged from the first public release in 2017 in botanical composition, but the 2019 forest conditions produced a gin with unusual warmth and balance.

The Standard

No Expression Leaves the Still Without Ailsa's Approval

Every Silvardin harvest is tasted blind against all previous vintages before bottling. If the 2024 expression does not meet or exceed the 2023, the bottles are set aside for a private cellar — not labelled, not released.

This has happened once in thirteen years: the 2018 harvest was affected by a contaminated water source. The entire batch was withheld. No harvest was released that year. Registered members were told the truth.

The standard is not marketing. The standard is the reason there is a harvest list at all.
