About Ignis

"We do not make candles. We design the quality of darkness in a room. The flame is our material — the wax merely gives it form."

Lena Voss, Founder, IGNIS ATELIER — Antwerp, 2018
The Origin

Light as a
Material.

IGNIS ATELIER was founded in Antwerp in 2018 by Lena Voss, a former lighting designer who spent a decade specifying artificial light for museum interiors and luxury residences. She grew increasingly frustrated that the most sophisticated lighting environments she created were undone the moment someone placed an ordinary candle on a table. The flame — the most ancient light source — was being ignored as a design element.

The atelier began as an experiment: could a candle be engineered to produce a predictable quality of light? Not just a pleasant scent and a warm glow, but a specific atmospheric condition — the stillness of a library, the animation of a dinner table, the intimacy of a bedroom? The answer, after three years of wax formulation and wick experimentation, was yes.

IGNIS now produces six candle typologies, each defined by its flame behaviour rather than its scent. Every scent decision begins with the question: what quality of light does this room need? The fragrance comes second. The flame is always first.

The Philosophy

Three Principles of Flame.

I

Flame First

Every IGNIS candle is specified by its flame behaviour before its scent is chosen. A Still flame requires a different wick diameter and wax blend than a Dancing flame. The atmospheric quality of the light — still, animated, or complex — is the primary design decision. Fragrance is secondary.

II

Small Batch

No IGNIS batch exceeds 60 pours. Each candle is hand-poured, hand-labelled, and hand-numbered. This is not a branding decision — it is a quality constraint. We cannot maintain the consistency of wax temperature, wick positioning, and pour timing that defines our product at higher volumes. We have tested it. The results are not the same.

III

No Greenwashing

We use pure beeswax and coconut wax, sourced from European suppliers with full traceability. We do not label anything "eco" or "sustainable" — these are words that have been drained of meaning by mass-market candle producers. We simply do not use paraffin. We do not use synthetic fragrance bases. We make the candles we would buy ourselves.

The Story

Ignis, from the Beginning

2018

The First Pour

Lena Voss converts the workshop attached to her Antwerp apartment into a candle laboratory. The first six months are spent exclusively on wick research — testing over 40 wick configurations with three wax bases to understand the relationship between wick diameter, wax composition, and flame behaviour. The first three candles are given to friends. All three are asked where to buy them.

2019

Flame Typology Published

Lena publishes a 12-page document titled "A Typology of Flame Behaviour" — not for marketing purposes, but as a working framework for her own design decisions. It is shared with a small lighting design community and generates significant interest outside the candle industry. The document becomes the conceptual foundation for the IGNIS product range and is still available on request.

2021

First Gallery Commission

A Brussels gallery commissions IGNIS to produce twelve candles for the opening of a photographic exhibition. The brief: the candles must not draw attention to themselves. They must extend the quality of the exhibition lighting after the track lights are dimmed. IGNIS delivers twelve Still-type candles in a neutral beeswax formulation. The gallery director asks for a standing arrangement for all future openings.

2023

Antwerp Atelier Opens

IGNIS moves from the apartment workshop to a dedicated atelier on Kloosterstraat in Antwerp's design quarter. The new space includes a production studio, a testing room with controlled humidity and airflow for flame behaviour observation, and a small reception area for atelier visits by appointment. Batch No. 001 from the new atelier is a limited edition of 20 candles, distributed only to the first 20 inner circle members.
