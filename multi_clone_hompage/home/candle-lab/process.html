Candle Craft

The Method

Everything Begins with the Wick.

A candle's flame behaviour is determined before the first drop of wax is poured. The wick — its diameter, braid type, length above the wax, and material — is the most consequential single decision in candle design. We spend more time on wick selection than on any other aspect of production. The wax formulation, the vessel, and the fragrance follow from the wick decision, not the other way around.

Five Stages

From Concept to Candle

I

Flame Typology Specification

Every new candle begins with a flame typology specification — a written description of the quality of light the candle must produce. This document answers: what room is this candle for? What time of day? What activity? What is the desired atmospheric quality? From this specification, we derive the target flame height, movement behaviour, and light spread. The fragrance brief comes after. A candle that produces the wrong quality of light is not corrected by an interesting scent.

Written spec before any pour
II

Wick Research

We maintain a library of approximately 60 wick configurations — different diameters, braid types, and core materials. For each new candle, we conduct systematic burn tests across candidate wicks in prototype vessels, observing flame height, movement, and mushrooming (carbon buildup). Each test is recorded with photographs at 2, 4, 8, and 24 hours. We rarely finalise a wick in under 3 weeks.

60+ wick configurations · 3-week minimum
III

Wax Formulation

Once the wick is specified, we formulate the wax to complement it. Pure beeswax produces a harder, slower-burning candle with a stable flame — appropriate for Still-typology candles. Coconut wax blends produce a softer, more responsive pool — appropriate for Dancing and Complex candles. The formulation is tested against the target wick in the target vessel before fragrance is added. Fragrance affects burn behaviour; we need a clean baseline first.

Beeswax or coconut blend · No paraffin
IV

Fragrance Integration

Fragrance is added to the wax at the minimum concentration that produces a perceptible scent throw. We resist the industry convention of maximising fragrance load — high fragrance concentrations interfere with burn behaviour and often produce a sweetness that obscures the natural beeswax scent. All IGNIS fragrances are built from natural base materials: essential oils, absolutes, and plant resins. We do not use synthetic fragrance ingredients.

Natural bases only · Minimum effective concentration
V

Hand Pour and Quality Check

Each candle is hand-poured in a single batch of maximum 60 units. Wax temperature at pour is 65–70°C for beeswax and 55–60°C for coconut blends — temperatures that produce a smooth surface and consistent fragrance distribution. The wick is positioned and held with a wick centering bar for 30 minutes. Each candle is inspected before labelling. Any candle with an off-centre wick, surface crack, or visible void is removed from the batch. Label and number are applied by hand.

Max 60 units · Hand-numbered
Kloosterstraat, Antwerp

The Studio.
Where Light is Made.

The IGNIS atelier on Kloosterstraat in Antwerp's design district was designed around two requirements: consistent temperature and controlled airflow. Both conditions affect flame behaviour during testing and therefore affect every production decision we make.

The space is divided into three zones. The production studio, where all pouring takes place at a controlled ambient temperature. The testing room, a sealed environment with regulated airflow for systematic flame observation. And the reception area, where atelier visits take place by appointment.

  • Temperature-controlled production studio: 20°C ± 1°C year-round
  • Sealed testing chamber with calibrated airflow control
  • Wick library: 60+ configurations on permanent display
  • Wax formulation archive: records from every batch since 2018
  • Small-batch fragrance mixing station with natural raw materials
  • Atelier visits by appointment: maximum 4 visitors per session
