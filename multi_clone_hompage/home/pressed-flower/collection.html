The Current Collection

Specimens Available for Acquisition.

Every piece in the HERBA CARTA collection is unique. When a specimen is acquired, it leaves the catalogue. It cannot be restocked — the moment it represents cannot be repeated. What is listed here is what exists. Each piece is presented with its full specimen documentation, and that documentation travels with it.

Cabbage Rose

Full bloom specimen collected at peak petal expansion. The layered petals compress into an extraordinarily complex record of their own structure. Mounted on 300gsm conservation board with linen backing.

The feathered bracts surrounding the flower press intact, creating an intricate lace-like surround to the central bloom. One of the most technically demanding specimens to press without disturbing the bract arrangement. Somerset cottage garden collection.

Specimen No. 019 · 2024
English Lavender
Lavandula angustifolia
Full stem with flowering spike pressed at the moment of maximum colour saturation, before the individual florets have opened. The purple-grey tones retain exceptional depth. From the Alentejo field collection — Portuguese lavender has a larger flower head than English varieties.
Collected: 03 May 2024 · Alentejo, Portugal
Press duration: 30 days · Diptych
Specimen No. 024 · 2025
Christmas Rose
Helleborus niger
Collected in January at the point of first opening — an unusual winter specimen. The white petals press to a pale ivory with a green-grey centre. The sepals retain their structure better than most specimens after pressing. From a private garden in the Mendip Hills with the owner's permission.
Collected: 18 Jan 2025 · Mendip Hills
Press duration: 42 days · Single frame
Specimen No. 031 · 2025
Common Poppy
Papaver rhoeas
The most technically challenging specimen in the collection. Poppy petals are so thin that they become almost transparent after pressing, and they are prone to tearing. When pressed successfully, however, they produce the most luminous result in botanical pressing — the tissue-thin scarlet petal holds light in a way no other specimen can match.
Collected: 08 June 2025 · Cornwall
Press duration: 14 days · Quad
Archival Standards

Materials That Outlast Their Owners.

Every HERBA CARTA piece is produced to museum conservation standards. The goal is simple: the specimen should be in the same condition in a hundred years as it is today. This is achievable. It simply requires the correct materials used correctly.

We do not use cheaper materials where museum-grade equivalents exist. We do not use adhesives that yellow or off-gas. We do not use backing boards that contain acid. We do not use glass that distorts colour. This is not a premium offering — it is the minimum standard for an object presented as permanent.

Backing Board100% cotton rag, pH neutral, 300–500gsm
AdhesiveMethylcellulose paste, archival grade
GlassMuseum anti-reflection glass, UV filtering
FrameSolid oak or walnut, unfinished or beeswax-treated
Specimen CardArchival inkjet on cotton rag paper, signed
