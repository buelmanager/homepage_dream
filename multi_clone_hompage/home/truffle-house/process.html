How We Work

The Grading Ritual

Method

From Forest to Atelier

Before any truffle leaves Terrae Noire, it passes through a five-stage evaluation process developed over three decades of practice. No instruments, no shortcuts — only trained hands, trained noses, and the absolute refusal to accept the ordinary.

I
Dawn Harvest

Our foraging partners work exclusively at dawn, when soil temperature and humidity are optimal for scent detection. Dogs, never pigs — trained animals who locate without damaging the mycelium network that will yield next season.

II
Immediate Wrapping & Transport

Each specimen is individually wrapped in paper — never plastic — and transported to our atelier within 24 hours of extraction. Temperature-controlled, never frozen. Freshness is not preserved; it is protected.

III
Sensory Evaluation

On arrival, each specimen is evaluated by at least two master graders — independently. Aroma intensity and character, surface firmness, visual integrity of the peridium, and internal marbling are assessed against our proprietary five-tier system.

IV
Grade Assignment & Documentation

A harvest certificate is issued for each specimen: grade, species, forager, date, region, weight. This document accompanies the truffle to the chef's kitchen and is retained in our archive indefinitely.

V
Placement & Guidance

We do not simply dispatch. We recommend application — which grade suits a raw service, which should be used in an infusion, which is worthy of tableside shaving. The chef receives not just a truffle, but an opinion.

Tools & Materials

The Grader's Kit

Our master evaluators work without instruments. The only tools are those carried within — decades of accumulated sensory memory.

Where tools are used at all, they are minimal: a precision balance calibrated to 0.1g, a magnifying loupe for surface inspection, and archive-grade paper for wrapping.
