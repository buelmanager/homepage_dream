Species & Grades

Seven Varieties, Four Regions

Each variety in our portfolio is sourced from a single region, evaluated by hand, and graded on arrival. No two seasons are alike. No grade is awarded that has not been earned.

Tuber melanosporum
Black Périgord

The queen of winter truffles. Harvested Nov–Mar from the oak forests of Périgord Noir. Deep, smoky, unmistakable.

Tuber magnatum
White Alba

Impossible to cultivate, impossible to replicate. Sourced from Umbria and Alba, Oct–Jan. A perfume that commands a room.

Tuber borchii
Bianchetto

The spring truffle of Istria. Feb–Apr. Lighter in aroma than magnatum, but radiant with white garlic and raw almond intensity.

Tuber aestivum
Summer Truffle

May–Aug. Herbaceous, restrained, deeply nutty. The truffle of patience — favoured by chefs who believe in subtlety.

Tuber uncinatum
Burgundy Truffle

Autumn, Sep–Nov. A cousin of aestivum with a more intense interior — earthy, fungal, and warming in cold-season preparations.

Preserved
Truffle Condiments

Preserved black truffle, truffle salt, and truffle oil — produced in-house from Réserve-grade specimens. Available year-round by allocation.

Our Standard

Quality Above
All Seasons

Every variety in our portfolio is graded on five criteria: aroma intensity, firmness, skin integrity, internal marbling, and harvest date. A specimen that fails on any single criterion is rejected — regardless of market scarcity.

We do not negotiate grade against availability. When the forest yields less, we offer less. The integrity of Terrae Noire has no seasonal exception.
