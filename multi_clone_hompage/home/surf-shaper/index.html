The Interview

Five questions before we touch foam

Every board starts as a conversation. We do not make shapes from size charts. We make shapes from wave knowledge — yours and ours combined.

Question 01
Where do you surf more than anywhere else?
Your home break determines the template. Beach break needs foam volume for unpredictable lips. Point break rewards narrow tails and drawn-out rails.
Question 02
What does your worst session feel like?
More useful than your best session. The limitations your current board imposes tell us exactly where we need to build different.
Question 03
Which boards have you loved? Which have you hated?
Loved boards reveal your instincts. Hated boards reveal your style — what your body wants to do that bad design prevented.
Question 04
How do you surf when no one is watching?
Pure surf style, no performance anxiety. We design for the surfer you are, not the surfer you think you should be.
Question 05
What do you want to feel on the wave?
Speed, projection, control, looseness, connection — every surfer weighs these differently. This answer sets the design priority hierarchy.

Materials Lab

Three materials. Three entirely different rides.

Material 01
PU / Polyester
Traditional — Standard Lead Time
Polyurethane foam with polyester resin. The classical combination. Heavier than EPS, more flexible under stress. The material that defined the modern shortboard era — responsive, tactile, familiar.
Flex · Responsiveness · Tradition
Material 02
EPS / Epoxy
Modern — Standard Lead Time
Expanded polystyrene with epoxy resin. Lighter, stiffer, more buoyant than PU. Ideal for performance shapes requiring speed without sacrificing paddle power. The choice for warm water and small surf.
Lightness · Speed · Buoyancy
Material 03
Balsa Wood
Heritage — Extended Lead Time
Hand-planed paulownia or balsa core. The most time-intensive build in the range. A board that responds to the ocean with organic sensitivity — flex patterns that cannot be replicated in foam and resin.
Organic Flex · Heritage · Weight
