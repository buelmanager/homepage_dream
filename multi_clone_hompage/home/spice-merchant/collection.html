The Selection

Twelve Origins. One Standard.

Each spice in the SAFFRANIA catalogue represents a single-origin lot from a documented farm. The selection changes by harvest year. What you receive is dated, graded, and traceable to the field where it grew.

Crocus sativus
Khorasan Saffron

Grade A ISO 3632 saffron from the Khorasan plateau. Two hundred thousand flowers yield one kilogram. The most labour-intensive crop in the world. Ours is tested for safranal and crocin before it leaves Iran.

Origin: 35.68° N, 59.22° E · Harvest 2024 · 2g tin
Rhus coriaria
Gaziantep Sumac

Hand-dried whole berries from Gaziantep, Turkey, harvested before full ripeness for peak tartness. Stone-ground to order. The citric sharpness degrades after eighteen months — ours is always fresh-ground.

Origin: 37.06° N, 37.38° E · Harvest 2024 · 80g jar
Elettaria cardamomum
Kerala Green Cardamom

Grade 1 green cardamom from Idukki district, Western Ghats. Picked at 75% ripeness for maximum aromatic oil content. Each pod inspected before curing. Cineol and terpinyl acetate levels tested per batch.

Origin: 9.91° N, 77.10° E · Harvest 2024 · 50g jar
Cinnamomum verum
Ceylon Cinnamon, Alba Grade

The rarest cinnamon grade, harvested from inner quill only. Delicate, sweet, and low in coumarin compared to cassia. From Matara district, southern Sri Lanka. Whole quill — never ground in advance.

Origin: 5.95° N, 80.55° E · Harvest 2024 · 40g tin
Syzygium aromaticum
Zanzibar Cloves

Whole dried cloves from Pemba Island, Zanzibar — once the world's dominant clove producer. Picked by hand before the flower bud opens. Essential oil content measured at 18%, above industry standard.

Origin: 5.25° S, 39.73° E · Harvest 2024 · 60g jar
Curcuma longa
Erode Turmeric

From Erode, Tamil Nadu — the largest turmeric market in Asia. Our lot tests at 6.2% curcumin content, well above the 3% industry average. Grown without chemical fertilisers on red loam soil.

Origin: 11.34° N, 77.72° E · Harvest 2024 · 100g jar
Quality Standard

Laboratory Graded. Every Lot.

Every shipment we receive is tested by an independent food chemistry laboratory before it reaches our atelier. We apply stricter standards than the receiving country requires. We reject approximately thirty percent of what we initially source.

The result is not perfection — it is consistency. When you buy SAFFRANIA saffron in 2024, it will perform the same as the saffron you bought in 2022. This requires work that most spice merchants do not consider worthwhile. We consider it non-negotiable.
