From Hive to Jar

The Art of Cold Apiculture

The Method

Five Steps, No Shortcuts

I
Step One — Spring

Wild Foraging & Bloom Mapping

Each spring, our apiarists survey a 3km radius around every hive site, documenting bloom succession from first snowmelt through peak summer. This living botanical map determines when each hive is in its optimal harvest window — not the calendar, not the market.

Typical duration: 6–8 weeks of observation before harvest decision
II
Step Two — Summer Peak

Ethical Frame Selection

Only fully capped frames — indicating complete enzymatic conversion — are selected for extraction. A minimum 30% honey surplus is left undisturbed in every hive as winter provision. We inspect each frame individually. There is no quota, no minimum yield, no pressure to take more than the colony can give.

Selection rate: typically 40–60% of available frames accepted
III
Step Three — The Atelier

Cold-Radial Extraction

Selected frames travel to the atelier within four hours of removal. The extraction room is maintained at 22°C maximum. Frames are spun in temperature-controlled stainless radial centrifuges — no heat assistance, no wax melting, no pressure. The honey flows at its own pace. No deviation from this protocol has ever been made.

Maximum room temperature: 22°C. Maximum honey temperature: 35°C
IV
Step Four — 48 Hours

Gravity Settling & Laboratory Analysis

Extracted honey settles in sealed stainless tanks for 48–72 hours. Air bubbles and residual wax particles rise naturally to the surface and are skimmed — no filters, no pressure clarification. Simultaneously, a 200g sample from each batch is submitted to our on-site laboratory for full biochemical profiling.

Analyses run: diastase activity, HMF, moisture, pollen spectrum, pesticide screen
V
Step Five — The Final Act

Hand-Fill & Wax Seal

Each jar is filled by hand on a calibrated filling station, weighed to ±2g accuracy, and sealed with our signature beeswax-and-resin blend. The cork is hand-pressed. The wax is embossed with a hexagonal stamp bearing the batch number and hive GPS coordinates. The label is applied by hand. Each jar is signed by the extracting apiarist.

Average time per jar: 4.5 minutes of hands-on work
Equipment & Materials

Tools Built for Integrity

⬡

Langstroth Hives

Our hives are constructed from untreated pine with beeswax-coated interiors. No synthetic treatments, no plastic foundation sheets. The bees build on natural wax starter strips.

◎

Radial Centrifuge

Custom-built 36-frame stainless radial centrifuge operating at variable speed, 0–280rpm. Temperature-logged throughout every extraction run. Calibrated quarterly.

▽

Settling Tanks

304-grade food-contact stainless settling tanks with coarse mesh gates (500 micron). Zero pressure assistance — gravity only. The only filtering in our process.

◇

Hand-Blown Glass Jars

Bespoke mouth-blown borosilicate glass jars, produced in small runs by an atelier in Alsace. Each jar is slightly unique — a deliberate choice that honours the hand-crafted nature of the honey itself.
