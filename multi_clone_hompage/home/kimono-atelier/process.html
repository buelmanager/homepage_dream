Our Process

The Commission Journey

Five stages that cannot be hurried

A KANADE kimono is not manufactured. It is cultivated — through conversation, through material selection, through the accumulated weight of a weaver's lifetime. The timeline ranges from one to three years. We tell clients this on the first meeting.

一
The First Conversation
第一回相談 — Initial Consultation

We meet at our Kyoto atelier or our Paris room, and we listen. Not to a brief — kimono clients rarely arrive with one — but to the occasion, the season, the memories the garment must carry. This conversation may last one hour or an entire afternoon.

Duration: 1–3 hours · In-person only
二
Textile Selection
素材選定 — Material Determination

We present fabric samples from our Nishijin suppliers — lengths of woven silk, chirimen crepe, ro gauze. You touch them, hold them to the light, feel their weight against your wrist. No selection is made until you have sat with the candidates for at least one day.

Duration: 1–4 weeks · Samples provided for home consideration
三
Pattern Design & Approval
図案設計 — Design Development

Our design team — which consists of exactly two people — develops the pattern in ink on washi paper. Revisions are made until the design carries the feeling of the conversation precisely. This stage involves minimum three rounds of presentation.

Duration: 2–6 months
四
Weaving & Dyeing
織染 — The Making

The longest stage. Nishijin-ori weaving proceeds at 3–5 centimetres per hour on heritage Jacquard looms. Dyeing may occur before weaving (yarn dyeing), after (piece dyeing), or both. Some commissions require 400 hours of loom time alone.

Duration: 6–24 months depending on technique
五
Fitting, Lining & Delivery
仕上げ — The Completion

The completed cloth is measured, lined, and sewn to the client's measurements in the traditional hon-shitaté method. A final fitting is held before the kimono is wrapped in washi, folded into paulownia wood, and delivered by hand.

Duration: 4–8 weeks · Delivered to your door
The Tools

Instruments of the slow art

Our milliners and weavers work with tools that in some cases predate the atelier itself — acquired from retired weavers, inherited from teachers, or commissioned from specialist makers.

機
Nishijin Jacquard Loom

1930s mechanical Jacquard looms, maintained by our in-house loom keeper. Capable of patterns up to 5,000 warp threads.

針
Kyoto Sewing Needles

Hand-ground #10 needles from Misuya in Nishiki Market — the same supplier since 1978. Three boxes consumed per year.

染
Natural Dye Vats

Clay-lined indigo vats maintained at constant temperature. Our oldest vat has been alive and fermenting since 1988.
