Menu

The Current Programme

The winter menu.
Renewed quarterly.

Verdigris's cocktail programme is reviewed and rebuilt four times a year. No drink shown here is guaranteed to be on the menu on your next visit. This is not an oversight. It is the central commitment of the bar: if the best available ingredients have changed, the drinks change with them. What follows is the current winter menu, correct as of this publication. Prices are not listed. The experience is fixed-format; reservation includes all service.

Winter Programme 2024 · Six Signatures
Copper Series · No. 01
The Patina
Verdigris house gin — copper-pot distilled, botanicals selected for reactivity — with dry vermouth that has been rested in a copper flask for seventy-two hours. Served over a single cast ice sphere in a copper cup. The drink changes slightly over the twenty minutes of service.
House gin · Copper-rested vermouth · Lemon oil · Cast ice
Reduction Series · No. 02
Sulphate
A sour built around copper sulphate's role in wine production. White Burgundy, copper-distilled pear brandy, and a citric acid solution calibrated to 2.4 pH. Finished with an egg white foam stabilised with locust bean gum. The blue tint in the base is natural and harmless.
White Burgundy · Pear brandy · Citric solution · Egg white · LBG foam
Cellar Series · No. 03
Roquefort
A savoury cocktail designed around oxidative aging. Manzanilla sherry, twelve-year Speyside Scotch, a fat-washed butter clarified in a copper pan at 160°C, and a reduction of late-harvest Sauternes. Named not for the cheese but for the cave — the darkness and the patience.
Manzanilla · 12yr Speyside · Copper-clarified butter · Sauternes reduction
Acid Series · No. 04
Verdigris Sling
The bar's most approachable drink. House gin, clarified lime juice, a sugar syrup made with copper-filtered water, and still water from the bar's reverse-osmosis system. Long, cold, and technically complex despite appearing simple. The complexity is in the water.
House gin · Clarified lime · Copper-water syrup · Still water
Botanical Series · No. 05
Corroded Garden
A botanical drink exploring the copper-tannin reaction. Cold-infused green tea, Darjeeling first flush, a tannin-heavy biodynamic Cabernet reduction, and a copper-distilled tequila from a single agave producer in Oaxaca. Served at 8°C in a terracotta cup treated with copper oxide.
Oaxaca copper-still tequila · Darjeeling infusion · Cabernet reduction · Terracotta service
Winter Series · No. 06
The Long Oxidation
A dessert drink served as the final course of the evening. A Calvados aged in copper-lined casks for eleven years, blended with a pear eau-de-vie distilled in-house, and a honey sourced from a beekeeper whose hives are sited near copper-rich soils in the Loire. The last drink of the night, and the one most people remember.
11yr copper-cask Calvados · House pear eau-de-vie · Loire copper-soil honey
The Format

Fixed format.
No exceptions.

Verdigris operates on a fixed-format service. All reservations include six drinks — the full current menu — served across approximately two and a half hours. There is no à la carte selection. You will drink what is on the menu in the order it is designed to be drunk.

This is not inflexibility. It is the only way to serve a programme designed around chemical progression. The evening is structured as a narrative: the first drink is an aperient; the last is a conclusion. Drinking out of sequence would be like reading a book backwards.

Dietary requirements and alcohol restrictions are accommodated with notice at the time of reservation. Non-alcoholic pairings for each course are available and designed with equal seriousness to the cocktail programme.
