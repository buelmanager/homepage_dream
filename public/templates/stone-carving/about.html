Petra Soma — Stone Carving Atelier

About

— Our Belief
"Every block of stone has a statue inside it, and it is the task of the sculptor to discover it."

Petra Soma was founded on a single conviction: that stone carving is not fabrication but revelation. We do not impose form upon stone. We read the stone and remove what is not the sculpture. What remains was always there — waiting, in geological silence, for the chisel.

— Origin Story

Founded in Carrara, 1988

Marco Bellini arrived in Carrara in 1975 as a student at the Accademia di Belle Arti. He did not leave. After twelve years of study and apprenticeship under the master carver Aldo Fioretti, he established Petra Soma in 1988 with one intention: to practice stone carving at its highest level, without compromise.

The atelier takes its name from the Greek — Petra, stone; Soma, body. Stone as body. Body as stone. Permanent, mortal, and capable of beauty that outlives every human generation that witnesses it.

Sigrid Thorvald joined in 2001, bringing an architectural understanding of load and structure. Ryu Nakamura arrived in 2014, introducing an Eastern contemplative approach to form. Together, the three constitute Petra Soma's complete identity — three hands, one philosophy.

— What We Hold

Core Values

I.
Revelation over fabrication

The sculptor does not make the form. The sculptor removes everything that is not the form. This distinction is everything. We approach each commission as archaeologists, not architects.

II.
Time as the primary medium

We accept four to six commissions per year. Not because we cannot work faster — but because speed is the enemy of stone. Each millimetre removed cannot be returned. We do not negotiate downward on time.

III.
Permanence as responsibility

A properly carved work in granite or marble will exist for three thousand years. We approach every commission knowing that future generations — not yet born — will stand before what we have made. We do not take that lightly.

— Heritage

Milestones

1988
Atelier Founded

Marco Bellini establishes Petra Soma in the Apuan Alps, Carrara. First commission accepted: a life-size portrait bust for a private collector in Florence.

1996
The Oslo Memorial

A public commission — a monolithic standing stone in Sardinian granite for a memorial garden in Oslo. The work establishes Petra Soma's reputation for outdoor architectural permanence.

2001
Sigrid Thorvald Joins

Norwegian architect and structural engineer Sigrid Thorvald becomes senior partner, expanding Petra Soma's capacity for large-scale architectural commissions.

2014
Ryu Nakamura Joins — The Long Hall Frieze

Japanese carver Ryu Nakamura becomes junior partner. The same year, Petra Soma completes the Long Hall Frieze — 18 metres of continuous narrative relief in Carrara Statuario, the most demanding commission in the studio's history.
