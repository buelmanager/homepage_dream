Workshop

From Consultation to Water

Five stages.
No shortcuts.

Making a Pellucid board takes between eight and fourteen weeks, start to finish. This is not a production delay. Every stage shown here has a purpose, and each one contributes directly to how the finished board performs. Marcus works alone, which means nothing is delegated and nothing is rushed to meet a deadline he cannot honourably keep.

The Process
01
Stage One

The Consultation

Every commission begins here. Marcus meets with the client — at the workshop or at the break they intend to ride — for a minimum of two hours. The conversation covers wave type, ride style, board history, physical characteristics, and what the client's current board does well and does not. Marcus does not accept a commission until he has a clear picture of the wave. Some consultations result in a referral to another shaper. Both outcomes are considered successful.

Duration: 2–3 hours · In person · Free of charge
02
Stage Two

Template and Foam Selection

Following the consultation, Marcus produces a template outline on paper — drawn by hand, not by software. The template defines the board's planshape: nose width, waist, tail, and the curves connecting them. The foam blank is selected to match the intended volume and rocker profile. For EPS constructions, the blank is cut to order. For wood boards, Marcus personally selects the timber from his stored supply of hand-evaluated panels.

Duration: 1–2 weeks · Template drawing and material sourcing
03
Stage Three

Shaping

The shaping bay is a controlled space — temperature and humidity are monitored because they affect both foam and the shaper's ability to read the surface. Marcus shapes one board per day, beginning at first light. The process involves a planer for rough removal, hand planes for the rail and concave work, and a series of sanding screens to bring the surface to the finished level. The shaping session is not timed. It ends when the board is ready.

Duration: 1 full day · Single shaping session per board
04
Stage Four

Glassing

The shaped blank is laminated with fibreglass cloth and resin. Marcus uses a hand-lay-up process — cloth is wetted out by brush and squeegee, not by machine. The glass schedule is determined per board: heavier for heavy surfers and powerful waves, lighter for trim-focused longboards and smaller riders. After hotcoat and fill coat, the board is wet-sanded to final finish. Pin-line and colour work, if requested, happens here.

Duration: 4–7 days including cure time
05
Stage Five

Inspection and Delivery

Before delivery, every board is measured against its original template specifications and inspected for delamination, pressure points, and finish consistency. Marcus photographs the finished board from all angles and provides a written build record — dimensions, materials, glass schedule, and fin system — that stays with the board. Delivery is in person where possible. Marcus prefers to hand a board over at the client's local break, and when circumstances permit, to watch it in the water.

Duration: 1 day · Inspection, documentation, handover
Materials

Every material chosen
for a reason

Pellucid uses four core material systems. The choice is made during the consultation based on wave type, rider weight, intended use, and how the client relates to the physical object they are riding. Marcus can explain the difference between any two systems in terms a non-technical surfer will understand.

  • PU Foam / Polyester Resin
    The traditional construction. Heavier than EPS, more responsive underfoot, more repairable. Preferred for beach and reef breaks where a lively feel is important. Approximately 70% of Pellucid's output.
  • EPS Foam / Epoxy Resin
    Lighter and more buoyant than PU. Preferred for mid-lengths, longboards, and step-ups where float is more important than flex. More brittle when damaged but significantly lighter in the hand.
  • Balsa Wood Core
    Hand-selected panels from a Portuguese supplier Marcus has worked with since 2015. Each balsa core is uniquely grained. Heavier than foam constructions but materially rewarding. Six boards per year maximum.
  • Paulownia Wood Core
    Lighter than balsa with a tighter grain. Excellent for longboard constructions where weight matters. Harder to work cleanly but produces a board with exceptional surface life. Available on request.
Tools

The Shaping Bay

Marcus shapes in a purpose-built bay adjacent to the main workshop. The bay is sealed against dust from the workshop floor, temperature-controlled between 18°C and 22°C, and lit with four fluorescent tubes positioned to eliminate shadow from the rail line. Every tool is cleaned and stored after each session.
