About

Our Belief
"Mead predates wine by five thousand years. We reclaim that primacy — not through nostalgia, but through excellence that wine has never achieved."

Aurelum was founded on the conviction that mead — humanity's oldest fermented drink — had never truly been taken seriously as a luxury beverage. We intended to change that. Twelve years of work, six named apiaries, and a 19th-century stone cellar later, we believe we have.

Founder's Story

Founded in a Stone Cellar, 2011

Marco Benedetti spent seven years as a sommelier at restaurants in Lyon and Copenhagen before a single jar of Provençal wildflower honey changed the direction of his career. He spent two years researching historical mead traditions — Viking, Celtic, Greek — before founding Aurelum in a cellar beneath his grandfather's farmhouse in the south of France.

The cellar was built in 1887. It has never been modernised. Marco did not want to modernise it. He wanted to understand what happens when mead is left in stone silence at 12°C for two years. The answer was what he found in the first Reserve expression: something wine could not produce.

Aurelum currently sources honey from six named apiaries across four countries. Every batch is seasonal, single-source, and allocated. The meadery accepts no retail distribution. The tasting room — guided by the meadmaker who made the mead — is the only place where Aurelum can be purchased in person.

What We Stand For
I.
Named Apiaries Only

We do not purchase honey by the tonne from anonymous producers. Every honey in an Aurelum batch is sourced from a named, visited apiary — a specific hive, a specific keeper, a specific ecosystem. The honey's biography is inseparable from the mead's character.

II.
The Cellar is Not Optional

Every Aurelum release spends a minimum of 12 months in our 1887 stone cellar at constant 12°C. No accelerated aging. No temperature manipulation. The cellar is not a storage solution — it is the primary instrument of fermentation.

III.
Allocation over Distribution

Aurelum is not sold in shops or through distributors. Registered members receive direct access to each seasonal release. This is not exclusivity for its own sake — it is the only way to ensure that the mead reaches people who will honour it.

Heritage
2011
First Batch — Clos des Abeilles

Marco Benedetti ferments the first Aurelum batch in the 1887 cellar. 48 bottles of a traditional Provençal wildflower mead. Aged 18 months. Given only to friends and advisors for blind tasting against premier cru white wines.

2014
The Reserve Expression

Aurelum releases the first Reserve: Il Castagno chestnut honey from the Tuscan hills, aged 24 months in Hungarian oak. The first expression to achieve recognition outside France. Wine critics begin to pay attention.

2018
Tasting Room Opens

The Aurelum tasting room opens in the cellar — guided by the meadmaker. Sessions sell out within 48 hours of each announcement. The tasting room becomes the primary point of contact between Aurelum and its community.

2023
Six Named Apiaries

Aurelum completes its apiary network: Provence, Poland, Tuscany, Brittany, Scotland, and Hungary. Each apiary contracted exclusively for Aurelum production. No other meadery in Europe sources from the same combination of named apiaries.
