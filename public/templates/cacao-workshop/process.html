The Craft

How We Work

From Origin to Edition

Each Umbra bar is the product of decisions made at every stage from seed to wrapper — most of them made in correspondence with a farmer we know by name. The process below is the same for every origin, adjusted only in time and temperature to reveal what is already present in the bean.

01

Stage One

Origin Selection & Sourcing

The process begins up to two years before a bar reaches a client. Elena visits each origin personally at least once before committing to a sourcing relationship. Selection criteria include varietal genetics, fermentation infrastructure, altitude, and the farmer's willingness to share detailed harvest data. No broker intermediaries. No spot-market purchases.

02

Stage Two

Reception & Sorting

Beans arrive at the Zug atelier in hermetically sealed jute-and-foil sacks, accompanied by harvest and fermentation documentation. Each batch undergoes cut test and moisture analysis before acceptance. Any batch with fermentation irregularities above tolerance is returned. Passed beans are sorted by hand for size uniformity before roasting.

03

Stage Three

Roasting

Each origin is roasted on a Probat drum roaster at a profile developed through cupping trials specific to the varietal and fermentation character. Typical profiles range from 138°C for twelve minutes on delicate Criollos to 156°C for sixteen minutes on robust Forasteros. The objective is development of flavour precursors while preserving the volatile aromatics that distinguish the origin.

04

Stage Four

Grinding & Conching

Roasted beans are cracked, winnowed to remove husk, then ground in a Lehmann melanging drum with a measured quantity of unrefined cane sugar — the only added ingredient. Conch time is between 18 and 40 hours, determined entirely by flavour development during tasting. Shorter conch preserves fruit and acidity; longer conch develops smoothness and rounds tannins. No lecithin is used to alter viscosity.

05

Stage Five

Tempering, Moulding & Edition

Finished chocolate is tempered by hand on a marble slab at 27–28°C, then poured into a single 60-gram mould per bar. Bars rest for a minimum of 72 hours before demoulding. Each bar is inspected, stamped with a batch number, then hand-wrapped in archival paper printed with the origin notes, farmer name, harvest year, and the bar's position within the edition of 240. Every bar is signed.
