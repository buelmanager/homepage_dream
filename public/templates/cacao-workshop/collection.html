The Edition Archive

Bars as Portraits

Each Umbra bar is released once, from a single origin, in a numbered edition of 240. The flavour profiles below represent origins from our current and recent past. A commission places you in correspondence with a specific origin — a geography, a farmer, a season — and produces a bar that belongs to that story alone.

Peru · Chuncho · 1,450m

Alto Urubamba

A rare Chuncho varietal from a cooperative of four families in Cusco province. Flavour notes of dark cherry, dried fig, and a long caramel finish. 72% cacao.

Madagascar · Criollo · 840m

Sambirano Valley

The estate that defined Umbra's reputation. An extended 72-hour fermentation yields notes of red berry, jasmine, and white pepper with an uncommon brightness. 70% cacao.

São Tomé · Forastero · 320m

Ilha do Meio

An island terroir shaped by volcanic soil and Atlantic humidity. Pronounced tobacco and leather notes evolve into smoked plum and molasses. 78% cacao, bold and structured.

Ecuador · Nacional · 600m

Los Ríos Arriba

The endangered Nacional varietal, rescued by a single-family estate in the Los Ríos province. Floral and lightly earthy, with banana blossom and raw honey at the finish. 68% cacao.

Vietnam · Trinitario · 380m

Bà Rịa Terroir

A frontier origin with a savoury, almost umami character — pickled plum, green tea, and a grassy brightness that distinguishes Vietnamese cacao from all other regions. 75% cacao.

Haiti · Hispaniola · 200m

Grande Anse

Grown by women's cooperatives in Haiti's southwestern peninsula. A rich, low-acid profile with dried mango, allspice, and sweet wood smoke. 74% cacao, creamy and round.

Material Integrity

Two Ingredients.
No Concessions.

Every Umbra bar contains exactly two ingredients: cacao and unrefined cane sugar. The percentage shown on the label is the minimum threshold at which the origin's character can express itself without the sugar becoming structural. No lecithin. No vanilla. No additional cocoa butter.

The roast profile is set individually per origin, ranging from light city-roast for floral Criollos to fuller profiles for robust Forasteros. Conch time is determined by flavour development, not convenience — typically between 18 and 40 hours per batch.

Each bar is poured into a single mould — 60 grams — and rested for a minimum of 72 hours before packaging. The wrapper includes the farmer's name, altitude, varietal, harvest year, and fermentation protocol.
