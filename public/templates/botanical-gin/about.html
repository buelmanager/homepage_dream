About

Our Philosophy
"A gin of one place. One season. One memory. Once it's gone, it's gone."

Silvardin was born from a simple refusal — the refusal to make gin that could be produced anywhere, any time, in any quantity. We make one gin per year, from one place, with botanicals foraged in a single autumn window. Nothing is blended afterward. Nothing is adjusted.

Founder's Story

From Forest to Copper

Ailsa Menzies left a career in pharmaceutical chemistry to return to the Scottish Highlands in 2011. She had spent fifteen years studying aromatic compounds — the science of smell. She wanted to make something that smelled like coming home.

The Silvardin Forest Reserve — 32 miles of ancient birch, juniper ridge, and lochside meadow — had been in her family for four generations. It took three years of foraging, mapping, and testing before she made her first distillation. She still makes every batch herself.

The name is a portmanteau of the Scots Gaelic word for silence — siorraidheachd — and the forest that produces the gin. Silvardin. The silence of the forest. Every bottle holds a season of it.

What We Stand For
01
Single Origin

Every botanical in Silvardin grows within 32 miles of the distillery. We do not source internationally for core botanicals. If the juniper fails this year, the gin reflects that reality.

02
One Distillation

We run once per year. One decision, one result. There is no blending batch to batch, no second attempt if the season was difficult. Every bottle is an honest record of its autumn.

03
Strict Allocation

Silvardin is never available on shelves. Registered members receive first access in order of registration. When the allocation is gone, the harvest is finished. No reprints, no restocks.

Heritage
2008
The Forest Map Begins

Ailsa Menzies returns to the Highlands and begins three years of foraging surveys across the Silvardin Reserve, cataloguing 47 botanical species by seasonal aromatic density.

2011
First Distillation

The copper alembic — a 250-litre Portuguese still — is installed. The first Silvardin harvest runs for seven hours. 180 bottles are produced and given to friends and foragers who helped with the survey.

2017
Harvest List Opens

After six years of word-of-mouth allocation, the Silvardin harvest list opens publicly. The 2017 vintage sells out in 11 hours. The annual run cap is set at 312 bottles — the maximum the single still can produce.

2024
Harvest 2024 — Record Juniper Year

The finest juniper crop in the reserve's recorded history. Early frost followed by a long warm September produces berry oil concentration 40% above average. The 2024 expression is the most awarded in Silvardin's history.
