The Harvest
2024
Autumn — Silvardin Forest Reserve

Gathered between the first morning frost and the autumn equinox, each botanical was hand-foraged at peak aromatic density. The distillation ran for seven hours in a single copper pot still. Nothing was blended. Nothing was adjusted.

The Still

A single copper pot.
A seven-hour run.

The Silvardin still is a 250-litre Portuguese copper alembic, installed in 2017. There is no shortcut in copper distillation. The conversation between spirit and metal takes time.

We run once annually. One distillation, one decision, one result. If the batch fails, there is no second attempt. There is only next autumn.

Tasting Notes

Autumn 2024 Expression

Written in the silence after the first sip.

Nose
Fresh-cut pine and cold stone. A lift of floral elderflower followed by a ground note of wet earth and moss.
Palate
Juniper-forward entry with a surprising softness. Coriander warmth builds mid-palate; cucumber cools the finish.
Finish
Long. Cardamom lingers for minutes — camphor-green and slightly smoky. Then quiet, like a forest after rain.

Provenance

Every coordinate is a decision.

Highland Ridge
57.2441° N, 5.8374° W
Primary juniper grove. North-facing slope retains moisture and slows ripening — produces denser berry oils.
Loch Margin
57.1928° N, 5.9122° W
Elderflower and wild violet. The shallow soil here forces the roots deep — botanical intensity is unmatched.

Birch leaf and angelica root. Ancient birch canopy shades the floor — harvested in the brief autumn window.
