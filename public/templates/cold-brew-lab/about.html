About CRYO LAB

"Heat was the obvious answer. It was also wrong. Cold extraction at 2°C is not a compromise — it is the only temperature at which everything the coffee contains becomes available without distortion."

— Dr. Emi Nakamura, Founder, CRYO LAB
Origin Protocol

A Food Scientist's Obsession

Dr. Emi Nakamura spent seven years in pharmaceutical cold-chain logistics before she applied the same thinking to coffee. The insight was simple: heat extraction is a shortcut. It works — but it destroys compounds that lower temperatures would preserve.

CRYO LAB was founded in 2020 in a converted Bermondsey railway arch with a single prototype extraction vessel, a precision temperature controller, and a stack of peer-reviewed papers on coffee chemistry. The first commercial batch was 24 bottles. They sold in four hours.

Today, CRYO LAB operates from a purpose-built extraction facility in Bermondsey and ships to twenty-two countries. Every batch is still numbered. Every bottle is still traceable. The extraction protocol has not changed since Experiment 001.

DR. EMI NAKAMURA — FOUNDER
Core Principles

Three Variables We Control

01 / TEMPERATURE

2°C. Always.

Temperature is monitored via IoT sensor array every 15 minutes throughout extraction. Deviation beyond 0.3°C triggers a batch hold. We have discarded more batches than we have sold. This is a feature, not a bug.

02 / TIME

22 Hours. Not 21. Not 23.

The 22-hour window was determined through 61 documented experiments (EXP-001 through EXP-061). Below 21 hours: under-extracted bitterness. Above 23 hours: astringency markers appear. 22:00:00 is the protocol.

03 / TRACEABILITY

Every Bottle Numbered

Each bottle carries a batch number laser-etched into the glass. That number maps to a complete data record: bean origin, roast date, grind particle size, water TDS, extraction temperature log, and final TDS. Available on request.

Lab History

Experiments that Made CRYO LAB

2020
Founding · Bermondsey Railway Arch
Experiment 001

First extraction at 2°C using Ethiopian Yirgacheffe. 22 hours. A single temperature data logger. The resulting brew was served to five people. All five asked when they could buy it.

2021
Scale · First Commercial Batch
Batch 001 — 24 Bottles

First commercial batch produced on a hand-built extraction vessel. 24 nitrogen-flushed glass bottles. Sold through a single Instagram post. Sold out in four hours. The waiting list that followed lasted eight months.

2022
Research · The 61 Experiments
The Protocol Is Confirmed

After sixty-one documented extraction experiments — varying temperature, time, grind size, water mineral content, and pressure — the current protocol is finalised. No subsequent experiment has improved on it. The science is settled.

2024
Expansion · 22 Countries
Purpose-Built Extraction Facility

New extraction facility with IoT-controlled temperature arrays and nitrogen flush sealing. Batch capacity expanded to 847 units per run. International cold-chain delivery established to 22 countries. Every bottle still numbered.
