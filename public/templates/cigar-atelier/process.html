The Craft

From Seed to Smoke

The Five Stages

An Ancient Method

I
Stage One · Cultivation
The Seed & The Soil
Every Casa Obscura cigar begins in volcanic earth. Our seed library — maintained since 1947 — contains seventeen heirloom Nicotiana tabacum cultivars. We plant only under the new moon, following a tradition our founder brought from the Vuelta Abajo.

Topsoil is enriched with aged organic matter and rested for two seasons between plantings. Fertiliser is prohibited. The earth must give freely, or not at all.

Growing Period
90–120 days from transplant to harvest
II
Stage Two · Fermentation
The Pilón & The Patience
Harvested leaves are bundled into pilónes — large mounds that generate their own internal heat. Over months, nitrogen and ammonia exhale from the leaf. Harshness departs; complexity arrives.

Our cellar master turns each pilón by hand, regulating temperature between 90° and 110°F. A single degree of variance produces a different cigar. We have turned pilónes the same way for seventy years.

Fermentation Duration
18–36 months depending on leaf priming
III
Stage Three · Blending
The Architecture of Smoke
Our master blender treats each cigar as a structural problem: the filler is the skeleton, the binder the musculature, the wrapper the skin. Every layer must function independently and together.

We produce test blends in runs of twelve. These are smoked blind by a panel of five — never fewer — before any blend is approved. Most blends are revised eight to fifteen times before release.

Average Blend Development
14 months, 11 iterations
IV
Stage Four · The Roll
The Torcedor's Hands
Our torcedores train for a minimum of five years before touching a production cigar. The bunch must be packed uniformly — not too tight to restrict the draw, not too loose to collapse under the wrapper's tension.

Each cigar is measured, weighed, and drawn before it leaves the rolling table. Tolerance: ±0.2g. Draw resistance: 1.2–1.8 inches of mercury. Cigars outside tolerance are unrolled and rerolled.

Production Rate
Each torcedor rolls a maximum of 80 cigars per day
V
Stage Five · Final Aging
The Geneva Vault
Rolled cigars enter our Geneva vault for a minimum of eight months at 65–68% relative humidity and 16°C. The vault is maintained by hygrometer, not electronic control — a deliberate choice. Seasonal fluctuation produces depth that precision cannot.

Our oldest vault inventory dates to 1989. We do not rush what time improves.

Vault Conditions
65–68% RH · 16°C · Cedar-lined cedar cabinets
