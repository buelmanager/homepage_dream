The Maker

Philosophy

"A blade is not made. It is negotiated — between the smith, the steel, the fire, and the eventual purpose. Every variable matters. Every compromise leaves a mark. I make twelve blades a year because twelve is the number I can make without compromise."

— Marcus Volker, Master Bladesmith, Ignis Lama

Origin

Fire, Steel,
and Thirty Years

Marcus Volker trained as a toolmaker in Stuttgart, spent five years under Japanese master bladesmith Takeda Hiroshi in Seki, and returned to Europe to establish his alpine forge in 1991 — twelve hundred metres above the Bodensee, where the air is cold and the forge burns hotter for it.

For the first decade he made more blades than he should have — and he knew it. In 2003 he stopped accepting more than twelve commissions per year. Not for scarcity. Because twelve is the number he can make without rushing a single judgement from heat to quench to finish.

He has not exceeded twelve since. The waitlist is currently thirty-one months.

What We Stand For

Three Principles

12
The Limit

Twelve blades per year. Not a marketing decision — a craft decision. Beyond twelve, something begins to be hurried. Hurried steel fails. Twelve is the honest number.

01
Single Maker

Every blade at Ignis Lama is made by Marcus Volker, from stock selection to final edge. No apprentices touch the steel. No stages are delegated. The certificate of completion carries one name only.

∞
Lifetime Guarantee

Every Ignis Lama blade carries an unconditional lifetime repair guarantee. The blade may need regrinding. The handle may need work. We will do it. For free. For the life of the blade.

Heritage

Thirty Years at the Forge

1991
The Alpine Forge Opens

Marcus Volker establishes his forge at 1,240m in the Vorarlberg Alps. The location is chosen for its isolation, its access to mountain water for quenching, and the cold that keeps the forge temperature differential at its most productive.

2003
The Twelve-Blade Rule

After twelve years of escalating production, Volker limits Ignis Lama to twelve commissions per year and closes the enquiry list. The waitlist at the time: eight months. It has grown by an average of four months per year since.

2014
The Codex of Materials

Volker publishes the Ignis Lama Material Codex — a documented specification of every steel, handle material, and finish he will work with, including the provenance of each material source. The Codex has been revised three times since.

2024
31-Month Waitlist

Current commission waitlist stands at thirty-one months. The twelve-blade limit remains unchanged. Volker has declined multiple acquisition offers. The forge is not for sale.
