About the Atelier

"Full-grain leather is not a material. It is a record — of the animal, the tannery, the craftsman's hand, and every year of your life that follows."
Founder & Origin

Built on the certainty that leather improves with use

Erik Verak opened his first workshop in Stockholm's Södermalm district in 1991 with one sewing machine, a single hide from a tannery in Tuscany, and a refusal to make anything he would not carry himself.

Thirty years on, VERAK ATELIER is recognised as Scandinavia's most demanding leather goods house — operating by commission, maintaining a two-year waiting list for new clients, and training each new craftsperson for a minimum of three years before they work on a client piece.

Our Principles

Three things we will not negotiate

01
Full-Grain Only

We use only the top layer of the hide — the full grain. It is the strongest, most character-rich part of the leather. It develops a patina that no corrected-grain hide can replicate. We have never used bonded or split leather. We never will.

02
Hand Stitching

Every seam in a VERAK piece is saddle-stitched by hand using two needles and linen thread. Machine stitching is faster. It is also less secure — a single broken thread unravels the seam. A hand-sewn seam holds even if the thread breaks.

03
Lifelong Repair

Every VERAK piece is repairable for the lifetime of its owner. We maintain a repair programme with no service charge — you pay only for materials. A piece that cannot be repaired is a piece we have failed to make properly.

Heritage

Three decades of considered work

1991
The Workshop Opens

Erik Verak establishes his first workshop in Södermalm, Stockholm, with a single Pfaff sewing machine and a hide from Conceria Walpier, Tuscany.

2003
Commission-Only

VERAK moves to a commission-only model, ending all wholesale relationships and establishing a direct client registry.

2015
The Repair Archive

The VERAK repair archive opens — a database of every piece ever made, enabling precise material matching for lifetime restorations.

2022
New Atelier

The atelier relocates to a larger space on Bondegatan, incorporating a client consultation room and expanded archive storage.
