The Process

Commission Process

Five stages from brief to delivery

No machine at any point in the process produces a component that goes into a VERAK piece. Every cut, every stitch, every edge is done by hand. This is slower. It is also the only way to produce something that will outlast its maker.

01
Initial Consultation

We meet at the studio in Stockholm or via video for clients outside Scandinavia. We discuss the commission — not what you want us to make, but how you intend to use it, carry it, and live with it. From this conversation comes the brief.

In-person preferred · Video available · No charge
02
Hide Selection

We send physical hide samples — cut to 10×10cm — for you to hold, smell, and flex. Leather is a sensory material. You cannot choose correctly from a photograph. Most clients take two to three rounds of samples before deciding.

Samples sent by courier · Selection may take 2–4 weeks
03
Pattern & Prototype

We cut a paper pattern and in most cases a prototype in an inexpensive leather. You review the form, the proportions, the pocket configuration, the strap placement. Changes at this stage cost nothing. Changes after cutting the final hide cost considerably more.

Prototype: 2–4 weeks · Changes made until form is approved
04
Hand Construction

The final piece is constructed by a single craftsperson from first cut to final edge paint. We do not split production between workers. The maker who cuts your piece sews it, finishes it, and hands it personally to our quality reviewer before it leaves the studio.

Construction: 6–14 weeks depending on complexity
05
Conditioning & Delivery

Every VERAK piece is conditioned before delivery with beeswax cream applied by hand, allowed to absorb for 48 hours, and buffed. The piece arrives wrapped in cotton flannel, in an unbleached cotton bag, with a written care guide and your piece's entry in our archive.

Delivered worldwide by courier · Signature required
The Instruments

Tools that age alongside the leather

Our tools are not chosen for speed. They are chosen for the quality of cut, the precision of stitch, and the degree of control they give the craftsperson.

I
French Skiving Knife

A short-bladed knife used to thin leather at edges and joins. Our knives are resharpened weekly using a leather strop.

II
Stitching Chisels

Japanese-made chisels for marking stitch holes at 4mm pitch. Driven by mallet, never by machine — machine punching distorts the grain.

III
Edge Beveler & Creaser

The beveler removes the sharp corner from cut edges; the heated creaser sets a decorative line 2mm from the edge. Both operations are done freehand.
