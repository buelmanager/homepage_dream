Our Story

Philosophy

"We do not sell truffles. We convey terroir. The forest floor remembers every oak root, every winter frost — and those who have learned to wait receive something irreplaceable."

— Pierre-Antoine Beaumont, Founder, Terrae Noire

Origin

A Forest, a Dog,
and a Conviction

Pierre-Antoine Beaumont grew up in the Dordogne, where the oak forests of Périgord Noir have harbored black truffles for centuries. At nineteen, he accompanied his grandfather on his first dawn harvest — and never returned to any other life.

After a decade training under master négociants in Périgord, Umbria, and Alba, he founded Terrae Noire in 1992 with a singular purpose: to build a direct, traceable supply of the world's rarest fungi — placed only with the chefs who understood what they received.

Today, Terrae Noire works with thirty-two foraging families across four regions. Every specimen is evaluated by hand. Every placement is a decision, not a transaction.

What We Stand For

Core Values

I
Traceability

Every specimen leaves our atelier with its harvest certificate — forager name, date, region, and grade. Anonymity has no place in serious gastronomy.

II
Restraint

We source only what the forest yields with integrity. No year-round supply, no substitution, no inflation of grade. The truffle dictates the terms — not commerce.

III
Relationship

Terrae Noire does not distribute. It places. Every chef in our network is known to us personally. We guide pairings, recommend applications, and refuse misuse.

History

A Heritage of Patience

1992
Foundation

Pierre-Antoine Beaumont establishes Terrae Noire in Sarlat-la-Canéda, Périgord — with three foraging families and one principle: direct, unmediated provenance.

2001
Expansion to Umbria and Alba

The first Italian partnerships are formed with foragers in the Umbrian hills and the forests surrounding Alba, adding Tuber magnatum to the portfolio.

2009
The Five-Tier Grading System

After seventeen years of empirical evaluation, Terrae Noire formalises its proprietary grading system — from Prestige A.O.C. to Exception — the industry's most exacting standard.

2018
Michelin Recognition

Six Michelin-starred restaurants cite Terrae Noire as a primary supplier. The atelier is recognised by Le Fooding and FT How to Spend It as defining a new category of culinary provenance.
