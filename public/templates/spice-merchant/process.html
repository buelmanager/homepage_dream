Sourcing & Blending

Our Process

Five Steps Between the Field and Your Kitchen

Most spice companies buy from brokers who buy from exporters who buy from aggregators. We do not. Our process begins with a site visit to a specific farm and ends with a laboratory report. Between those two points are five stages we control entirely.

01
Relationship · Annual Visit

The Farm Visit

Every supplier relationship begins with a site visit. We assess soil, irrigation, harvesting method, and post-harvest handling before placing a first order. Ongoing suppliers are visited once per year, minimum. We do not accept spices from farms we have not seen. This eliminates a large portion of the global market — deliberately.

02
Harvest · Timing is Everything

The Harvest Window

Peak flavour occurs in a narrow window. Saffron must be harvested in the two hours after sunrise. Pepper must be picked at exactly 10% cluster ripeness for PGI standards. Cardamom at 75% pod maturity. We coordinate with farmers to ensure our lots are harvested at the correct moment — not at the convenient moment.

03
Transit · Cold Chain

Cold Chain Logistics

Heat and humidity are the primary enemies of spice quality in transit. We specify cold chain logistics for all our spices — particularly saffron and cardamom. Temperature logs accompany every shipment. We reject any lot where the cold chain was broken, regardless of cost.

04
Testing · Independent Laboratory

Laboratory Grading

Every shipment is tested by an independent food chemistry laboratory upon arrival in London. We test saffron for safranal, crocin, and picrocrocin against ISO 3632. Pepper for piperine. Cinnamon for coumarin. Turmeric for curcumin. Results are printed on the batch documentation available in every order.

05
Blending · Atelier · Bermondsey

The Bermondsey Atelier

Blending occurs in our Bermondsey atelier under controlled humidity and temperature conditions. Blends are composed by weight using ancient Persian mathematical ratios: 1 part dominant note to 4 parts accent to 0 parts filler. We do not use anti-caking agents. We do not add colour. We do not blend to a price point.

The Atelier

Tools of the Caravan

Our blending atelier is equipped with antique brass scales accurate to 0.1 gram. Every vessel is dark glass or hand-sealed tin — light degrades volatile oils faster than time. The packaging is part of the preservation protocol.

  • Antique brass scales, 0.1g precision
  • Airtight dark glass tins — UV filtered
  • Nitrogen flush sealing for premium lots
  • Temperature-controlled storage at 15°C
  • Hand-labelled with batch number and harvest date
  • Third-party lab certificates included per order
