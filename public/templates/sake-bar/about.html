KIRI — Our Story

The Maison

Brand Manifesto

"We opened KIRI because we believed London deserved a place where sake was treated not as a curiosity but as a civilization."

KIRI — from the Japanese 霧, meaning mist or fog — takes its name from the ethereal quality of the finest sake: its ability to transform the atmosphere of a room through presence alone, without ostentation. We believe the best sake does what the best music does: it makes you aware of time passing and grateful for it.

Our mission is simple but demanding: to serve only sake that rewards serious attention, in an environment designed to slow perception, curated by people who have devoted their professional lives to understanding the most ancient fermented tradition in the world.

Founder's Story

Hana Kimura

"I grew up watching my grandfather drink sake in silence every evening. It was not alcohol — it was a practice. That is what I want KIRI to be."

Hana Kimura was born in Kyoto and trained as a kikizake-shi (certified sake sommelier) before working with Nishiki Market's most respected sake merchants. She moved to London in 2012 to study the sommelier arts at the Court of Master Sommeliers, and returned to Japan annually to deepen her relationships with small-scale kura.

KIRI opened in 2015 with 80 labels and a singular conviction: that sake, presented with the same rigour as fine wine, could change how Europeans understood Japan's greatest agricultural tradition.

— Hana Kimura, Founder & Head Sakagura
Core Values

What We Practice

誠

Makoto — Sincerity

We present only what we truly believe in. No label enters our cellar without earning the conviction of our team through multiple blind tastings across different seasons.

間

Ma — Negative Space

Silence and pacing are as important to us as the sake itself. We resist the temptation to fill space with noise. The pause between pours is a gift.

深

Fukasa — Depth

We are not interested in easy sake. We seek complexity that rewards patience — labels that open across 30 minutes in the glass and surprise you at the close.

Our Heritage

Nine Years of
Devoted Presence

2015

Opening in Fitzrovia

KIRI opens with 80 labels, a 14-seat counter, and a commitment to omakase service at a time when sake was still largely unknown in London.

2017

First Brewery Partnership

Hana Kimura establishes KIRI's first direct import agreement with Niigata's Echigo Tsurume brewery — beginning a series of exclusive allocations.

2019

Paris Salon Opens

A small tasting room in the Marais introduces KIRI's curatorial philosophy to Parisian audiences for the first time.

2024

The Koshu Archive

KIRI opens Europe's first curated vertical archive of aged koshu — sake aged from 3 to 15 years — creating an unprecedented tasting experience.
