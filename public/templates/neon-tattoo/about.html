WHO WE ARE

Manifesto

WHAT WE BELIEVE

We believe that a tattoo is not decoration. It is a declaration — a permanent, public commitment to something that cannot be unsaid.

Voltage Ink was founded on the conviction that fine art and body art are not separate disciplines. The same principles that govern a great painting — composition, colour theory, line, light, shadow — govern a great tattoo. The difference is the canvas. And we believe the human body is the most compelling canvas there is.

We specialise in neon ink work not because it is fashionable but because we believe the electric light of the modern city is the defining visual grammar of our time. To tattoo with those colours — to put the night on the skin — is to make a statement about what era you inhabit and what it means to be alive in it.

We do not do flash. We do not rush. We do not compromise on materials. Every piece we make is made as if it is the only piece we will ever make. Most of the time, for the client, it is the most permanent mark they will ever wear. We take that seriously.

Founding Artist

RYO KESSLER

Founder & Lead Artist — Neo-Traditional / Neon Irezumi

Ryo Kessler trained for five years as a traditional Japanese irezumi artist in Kyoto before moving to Los Angeles in 2009, where he immediately began experimenting with UV-reactive inks as a way of bringing the electric nocturnal light of the city into the ancient craft he'd inherited.

His work — which he calls "neon irezumi" — takes the formal structures of traditional Japanese tattooing (composition, subject, iconography) and executes them in high-saturation and UV-reactive neon palettes. Under daylight, the tattoos read as vivid and unusual. Under blacklight, they transform entirely.

He founded Voltage Ink in 2012 with three other artists and has since assembled a collective of twelve, all of whom were selected as much for their conviction as for their craft.

The Process
What Drives Us

THREE CONVICTIONS

01

CUSTOM ONLY

We do not maintain a flash book. Every piece that leaves Voltage Ink was designed for a specific person on a specific body. We will never put the same design on two clients. The uniqueness of each tattoo is non-negotiable.

02

MATERIALS MATTER

All inks used at Voltage Ink are EU-certified for heavy metal content and allergen screening. Our UV-reactive inks are sourced exclusively from labs with published long-term safety data. We will not use an ink we cannot stand behind completely.

03

THE LONG VIEW

We design for how a tattoo will look in forty years, not just on the day. Neon saturation fades differently to conventional pigment — we account for this in every design, building in colour weighting that ensures the piece ages with intention, not chance.

History

FOUR CHAPTERS

2009
Arrival

TOKYO TO L.A.

Ryo Kessler arrives in East Los Angeles with five years of irezumi training and an idea: what if you tattooed ancient Japanese subject matter with the electric palette of the Blade Runner night sky?

2012
Foundation

VOLTAGE INK OPENS

The studio opens in a converted electrical substation on East 6th Street with four founding artists. The waiting list hits six months within the first ninety days of operation.

2018
Global

TOKYO RESIDENCY

A seasonal Tokyo residency opens in Shimokitazawa, creating a dialogue between Voltage Ink's neon aesthetic and the city that first inspired it. The residency runs three months per year.

2024
Present

12 ARTISTS, 6 CITIES

Voltage Ink now operates from Los Angeles, Tokyo, and through four annual international residencies. Twelve resident artists. An eighteen-month waiting list for Ryo's personal commissions.
