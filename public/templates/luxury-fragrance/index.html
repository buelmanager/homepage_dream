VELLUM was born from a single conviction: that the oldest sense deserves the most truthful attention. We macerate slowly. We distill carefully. We never synthesize what nature still yields. Each flacon contains an ecosystem — not an idea of one. This is not perfumery as status. This is perfumery as consequence.

The Atelier

Five Stages of
Alchemy

From field to flacon: the journey each VELLUM fragrance takes before it is ready to inhabit a life.

The maceration room — Antibes, France
01
Wild-Harvested Sourcing

Our perfumers travel to origin. We work directly with cooperatives in Grasse, Assam, Socotra, and the Atlas Mountains — buying at harvest, not through brokers. Raw material quality is non-negotiable.

02
Cold Maceration

Delicate materials — jasmine, rose, violet — are cold-maceated in organic carrier oils for a minimum of 21 days. This preserves volatile top notes that steam distillation destroys. No short cuts.

03
Accord Development

Our resident perfumer develops each accord over a period of 6–18 months. We iterate through dozens of structural variations before the formula is locked. Simplicity is pursued, never assumed.

04
Hand Filling & Sealing

Each bottle is filled by hand in our Antibes atelier. The crystal flacon is individually inspected, the stopper hand-polished, and the ribbon-sealed collar applied by a single artisan who signs the base.

05
Numbered Edition & Certificate

Every VELLUM fragrance is issued in editions of 500. Each flacon carries a hand-written edition number, batch date, and the perfumer's initials. A provenance certificate accompanies every purchase.
