Grove to Table

The Philosophy

Eight Hours from Tree to Press.

The interval between picking and pressing is the most critical variable in olive oil quality. Oxidation begins the moment the fruit leaves the branch. At Athenaion, we press within eight hours of picking — a constraint that requires the entire harvest operation to be planned with military precision and that no olive leaves the grove before midday without being in the press by nightfall.

Five Stages

The Harvest Process

I

Canopy Inspection

In the weeks before harvest, Eleni Athenaios walks every row of the grove, assessing ripeness by eye and by the feel of individual olives. The harvest date for each expression is set only after this inspection is complete. No fixed calendar — the trees decide the timing. In 2024, harvest was called eight days later than the previous year following a cooler September; in 2022, it was moved forward by four days.

September – October inspection
II

Hand Harvest

Every olive at Athenaion is hand-picked or harvested using hand-held electric combs — never mechanical shakers. The mechanical shake method is faster and cheaper, but it strips unripe and overripe fruit indiscriminately and bruises the olives, beginning oxidation before they reach the press house. Hand harvesting takes longer, costs more, and produces a cleaner fruit. There is no other method we will consider.

Hand-picked · No mechanical shakers
III

Immediate Transport

Harvested olives are placed in shallow perforated crates — never deep containers that would allow the weight of the fruit to crush the lower layers. They are transported to the press house immediately in the estate vehicles. The drive is four minutes. The olives are never stored overnight. If a day's harvest cannot be pressed by 22:00, the picking stops for that day.

Max 8 hours grove to press
IV

Cold Press

The olives pass through a leaf remover and washer, then into the stainless steel hammer mill. Malaxation — the slow mixing of the olive paste before pressing — takes place at 27°C or below and lasts exactly 25 minutes. Longer malaxation increases yield; we prefer the earlier stop. The oil is then extracted by centrifuge and filtered through a 150-micron screen. No chemicals. No heat. No additives.

27°C maximum · 25-minute malaxation
V

Rest and Bottling

The fresh oil rests in stainless steel tanks with nitrogen blankets for three to four weeks before bottling — long enough to allow natural sediment to settle, short enough to preserve the aromatic compounds that define the early harvest character. Bottles are filled, sealed, and labelled by hand. Each label is printed with the harvest date, expression name, batch number, and pressing house. The oil ships within two months of pressing.

3–4 weeks rest · Hand labelled
The Land

Limestone, Altitude, and the Afternoon Wind.

The flavour of Athenaion oil is inseparable from its geography. The grove sits on a ridge of Triassic limestone at 320 metres above sea level — a combination that produces the mineral character present in every expression we press.

Three conditions are responsible for the quality we achieve each year. They cannot be replicated elsewhere, and they require no intervention from us.

  • Limestone geology — the root systems of the oldest trees penetrate 18 metres into the rock, drawing mineral-rich water that cannot be replicated by surface irrigation. We do not irrigate. The trees have managed without it for three centuries.
  • Altitude and aspect — at 320 metres, the grove receives direct sun from 09:00 to 16:00 and then falls into shade. This cycle of intense light and relief prevents the fruit from overheating, preserving the volatile aromatics that make the oil complex.
  • The Meltemi — a seasonal dry wind from the mountains that keeps humidity low, reduces pest pressure without chemical intervention, and concentrates the flavour compounds in the fruit during the final weeks before harvest.
  • Koroneiki variety — the variety native to the Peloponnese, known for small fruit, high polyphenol content, and a robustly peppery character. We do not grow any other variety.
