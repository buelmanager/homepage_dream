About the Grove

"The trees do not belong to us. We belong to the trees. Three hundred years of pressing is not a tradition — it is an obligation to what came before."

The Lineage

Three Centuries.
One Family.

The Athenaion Estate was established in 1724 on the Laconian plateau of the Peloponnese, Greece, at an altitude of 320 metres above sea level. The first trees were planted by Nikolaos Athenaios, a landowner who recognised the exceptional terroir of the volcanic limestone ridge — its mineral-rich soil, the particular angle of the afternoon sun, and the dry winds that descend from the Taygetos mountains.

Seven generations of the same family have pressed every harvest. The estate produces a single expression each year, from Koroneiki olives harvested in a precise 14-day window before full ripeness — when phenolic compounds are at their peak and the oil is still green, bright, and assertive. No blending with other estates. No second pressing. One season.

The current generation is led by Eleni Athenaios, who modernised the pressing house in 2012 with stainless steel malaxation equipment — the only significant change to production since the 1920s. The grove itself has not changed at all. Three hundred years of the same trees, on the same hill, under the same Greek sky.

What We Stand For

Three Principles.
No Exceptions.

I

Single Estate

Every drop of Athenaion oil comes from our 1,200 trees on the Laconian plateau. We do not purchase fruit from other farms. We do not blend with other regions or varieties. The bottle contains exactly what the land produced that year, nothing more.

II

Early Harvest

We harvest in the third week of October, before full ripeness. The oil is greener, more complex, and higher in polyphenols than oils from later harvests. It is more demanding — a shorter window, lower yield — but it is the moment when the oil is most itself.

III

No Shortcuts

Cold pressing at 27°C or below preserves aromatic compounds that heat destroys. The malaxation process takes longer. The yield is lower. The quality is audibly different. We do not heat, dilute, re-press, deodorise, or blend. We document every stage of every pressing.

Three Centuries

A Chronicle of the Grove

1724

The First Planting

Nikolaos Athenaios plants the original 400 Koroneiki trees on a limestone ridge above the Eurotas valley. He records in his estate journal that the soil "smells of metal and salt" and that the olives are smaller than those in the valley below, but "more violent in character." Forty trees from this original planting are still producing fruit today.

1890

The Grove Expands

Third-generation patriarch Alexandros Athenaios plants an additional 800 trees on the northern slope, bringing the estate to its current 1,200-tree count. He introduces the practice of early harvest — a radical departure from regional convention that his neighbours consider foolish. Within a decade, Athenaion oil becomes known in Athens as a luxury product. The practice remains unchanged.

1961

International Recognition

A shipment of Athenaion oil reaches a Parisian gastronomy house through a trading acquaintance. The resulting word-of-mouth creates the estate's first international correspondence. Konstantinos Athenaios begins maintaining a waiting list for the annual harvest allocation. The model — limited production, direct allocation, no retailers — is born of necessity and becomes the estate's enduring identity.

2012

A Single Modernisation

Eleni Athenaios, the seventh generation, installs a stainless steel two-phase cold-extraction pressing house at the estate. She preserves every aspect of the harvesting and sorting protocols while improving press hygiene and temperature control. The following year, the 2013 harvest wins the first major international accolade in the estate's history. The grove itself is untouched.
