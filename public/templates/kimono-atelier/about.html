Our Story

About the House

"To weave a kimono is to slow time itself — to ask the hand to move with the patience of water shaping stone over centuries."
Founder & Origin

Born in the silence of the Nishijin loom rooms

KANADE was founded in 1974 in Kyoto's Nishijin textile district, where the sound of a thousand looms is so constant it becomes indistinguishable from silence. Founder Shizuka Nagano spent twelve years as an apprentice before creating her first kimono under her own name.

She believed that luxury was not ornamentation — it was time. Time spent learning, time spent weaving, time spent understanding what a patron truly needed to carry into the most important moments of their life. KANADE has not changed this belief in over fifty years.

Shizuka Nagano
Founder · 1974
Our Principles

Three commitments we have never broken

織
The Weave

Every KANADE kimono is woven in the Nishijin district using Jacquard looms that operate at a fraction of industrial speed. We are not in a hurry. The weave cannot be hurried.

染
The Dye

Our dyes are derived from plant and mineral sources — indigo, persimmon, safflower. We have refused synthetic dyes since our founding. The colour of a KANADE kimono changes subtly over decades. This is not a flaw. It is the garment living.

記
The Archive

Every commission is documented — the client, the occasion, the pattern selection, the yardage, the dye bath number. These records are maintained for a minimum of 100 years. We have found records of commissions from 1908.

Heritage

Five decades of silent mastery

1974
The Founding

Shizuka Nagano establishes KANADE in the Nishijin district of Kyoto with a single loom and three assistants.

1989
Imperial Recognition

A KANADE kimono is commissioned for the Enthronement ceremony of the Heisei era. The atelier declines to publicise the commission.

2004
Paris Expansion

A second consultation room opens in Le Marais, Paris — allowing European clients their first in-person KANADE experience.

2019
Second Generation

Shizuka's daughter, Hanako Nagano, assumes creative leadership while her mother remains as philosophical adviser.
