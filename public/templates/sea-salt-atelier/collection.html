Why Salinae Crystals Are Different

The difference between Salinae salt and commercial salt is not a matter of degree. It is a matter of kind. Commercial salt is a mineral isolate — sodium chloride at 99.7% purity, with virtually everything else removed. Salinae salt is a complete marine mineral complex — sodium chloride at approximately 85%, with the remaining 15% composed of magnesium, potassium, calcium, iodine, and 80 additional trace minerals.

The difference is measurable in the laboratory and unmistakeable on the palate.

  • 84 natural trace mineral elements retained in every crystal
  • Solar evaporation only — no industrial heat drying
  • No anti-caking agents, no iodine supplementation
  • Hand-raked and hand-sorted — no mechanical processing at the crystal
  • Full mineral analysis published for each batch
  • Harvest-date and site labelled on every vessel

Each crystal carries the chemical memory of a specific ocean. Discover which one speaks to you.
