Our Origins

The Philosophy of the Paludier

Manifesto

What We Believe

We believe that salt is the ocean's autobiography — a mineral record of depth, current, and geological time, compressed into a crystal that dissolves on the tongue in seconds.

Salinae was founded on a single conviction: that the world does not need more salt. It needs better salt. Salt harvested with patience. Salt that retains its full mineral complexity. Salt that carries the chemical fingerprint of the specific ocean it came from.

We do not compete on price. We do not compete on volume. We compete on a single dimension: the depth of mineral expression in every crystal we produce. That is the only measure by which we judge our work.

Salinae salt is harvested once per year, from three ocean sites. Each site produces a different crystal, with a different mineral profile, a different texture, and a different culinary character. They are not interchangeable. They are three distinct arguments about what salt can be.

The Founder

Margaux Delval

Master Paludier & Founder

Margaux Delval grew up in a paludier family on the Guérande peninsula — the same Breton coastline where Salinae's Atlantic harvest sites stand today. She trained as a marine chemist at the University of Brest before returning to salt harvesting with a question her professors couldn't answer: why did commercially processed salt taste of almost nothing?

The answer, she discovered, was mineral stripping. Industrial salt production removes 82 of the 84 natural trace elements that give sea salt its complexity. Margaux founded Salinae to prove that retention of those minerals was not just possible at artisan scale — it was essential to the product's identity.

She designed the twelve-pond gradient system that Salinae now uses at all harvest sites, and continues to manage the Atlantic harvest personally each summer.

Core Values

Three Commitments

01

Full Mineral Retention

We never wash, rinse, or refine our crystals after harvest. The 84 natural mineral trace elements in seawater remain intact. This is not a selling point. It is the only way we know how to make salt worth eating.

02

Ocean Provenance Transparency

Every jar is labelled with the harvest site GPS coordinates, the harvest date, and the name of the paludier who raked it. You know exactly which ocean, which day, and which hands produced the salt in your kitchen.

03

No Mechanisation at the Crystal

The crystallisation and harvest stages of our process involve no mechanical equipment. Rakes are wooden-handled. Sorting is done by hand under magnification. No machinery touches a Salinae crystal until it is placed in its glass vessel.

History

Four Defining Years

2016
Foundation

The First Atlantic Harvest

Margaux establishes the first twelve-pond gradient system on the Breton coast. The inaugural Atlantic Fleur de Mer harvest yields 340kg — sold entirely to three Paris restaurants within a week.

2018
Expansion

Sicily & the Mediterranean

A second harvest site opens near Trapani, Sicily, using the same gradient system adapted for the warmer, more saline Mediterranean. The Rose Crystal becomes Salinae's most requested product within its first season.

2021
Pacific

Okinawa Deep Water Reserve

In partnership with a traditional Okinawan salt farming family, Salinae establishes a third site harvesting deep Pacific water — mineralically distinct from the Atlantic and Mediterranean, with a higher magnesium and iodine content.

2024
Present

The Black Lava Reserve

A limited volcanic salt from Hawaii, produced using activated charcoal from coconut shells and Hawaiian deep Pacific water — the fourth and most dramatic expression in the Salinae archive. Only 200 jars produced annually.
