Founder & Origin

Born from a single obsession with the brim

In 1887, Édouard Vauclaire set his apprentice workbench along the rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré with nothing more than a rabbit-felt hood, a block of carved lime, and an impossible demand: that each hat he produced would outlive its wearer in elegance.

Three generations later, Maison Vauclaire remains the most quietly celebrated millinery house in the world — never advertising, never wholesaling, never compromising. The atelier in Paris's 8th arrondissement has remained in the same building since 1903. The hands have changed. The philosophy has not.

Our Foundations

Three principles that govern everything

I
Commission Only

We do not keep stock. Every hat is made for one person, shaped to their head, and delivered to them alone. This is not exclusivity as marketing — it is the only honest way to make a hat.

II
Material Integrity

We source directly from the three tanneries and two felt producers in Europe whose standards we trust unconditionally. We have never used synthetic binding. We never will.

III
Lifelong Relationship

Every commission enters our archive. Every client may return for reshaping, relining, or renewal. A Vauclaire hat is not a purchase — it is the beginning of a correspondence.

Heritage

A century and a third of mastery

1887
The Founding

Édouard Vauclaire opens his first atelier on the Faubourg Saint-Honoré with three apprentices and a singular vision.

1924
Royal Appointments

The house is granted millinery appointments to four European royal courts, cementing its standing as the definitive luxury milliner.

1972
Third Generation

Céleste Vauclaire returns from studies in Florence to take creative direction, introducing couture-level textile collaborations.

2009
The Archive Opens

Over 4,000 commission records are digitised and made available to clients. The physical archive remains on-site in the atelier.
