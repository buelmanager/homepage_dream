The Craft

Commission Process

Five stages from conversation to creation

No two commissions follow exactly the same path — but every hat at Maison Vauclaire moves through the same five essential stages. Understanding them helps you arrive at your appointment with confidence and leave with a hat that will endure.

01
Private Consultation

Your commission begins with a conversation at our Paris atelier. We discuss the occasion, the silhouette, the palette, and the feeling you wish to carry with you. There is no form to fill — only listening.

Duration: 60–90 minutes · By appointment
02
Head Measurement & Block Selection

We take twelve precise measurements of the head using instruments unchanged since 1903. Your personal block is then selected or carved from our archive of 3,400 blocks — or a new one commissioned from our block-maker in Lyon.

Precision to 0.5 mm · Lifetime block archived
03
Material Selection & Draping

We present you with the felt, sinamay, straw, or textile options appropriate to your brief. You handle the materials, hold them to the light, and feel their weight and drape before any decision is made.

Materials sourced from Lyon, Como, and London
04
Hand Construction

Each hat is built entirely by one milliner — from the first blocking through wiring, trimming, lining, and finishing. No hat passes between hands mid-construction. This continuity of touch is central to our quality.

40–120 hours of construction per hat
05
Fitting & Delivery

Before delivery, we invite you for a final fitting. Minute adjustments are made — to the crown height, brim angle, or ribbon tension — until the hat sits as it must: as though it has always been part of you.

Delivered in a cedar-lined hatbox · Archived in our records
The Instruments

Tools as old as the tradition

Our milliners work with instruments that have been in continuous use for generations. We acquire new tools reluctantly — and only when a technique demands it.

I
The Conformateur

A 19th-century head-shape recording instrument. Our 1891 Lefebvre conformateur has mapped over 2,000 heads.

II
Curling Irons

Hand-held irons heated over open flame, used to curve brims with a control that no machine can replicate.

III
Hat Blocks

Carved from lime wood by a single craftsman in Lyon. Each block represents a specific head geometry or silhouette.
