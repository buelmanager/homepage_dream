About URWA

"Lacquer does not hide the wood beneath it. It makes the wood visible for the first time — by giving it a surface that light can trust."
Founder & Origin

Established in the humidity of a Kyoto winter

URWA was founded in 1983 by Kenji Yamamoto in the Higashiyama-ku district of Kyoto — a neighbourhood of temples, narrow lanes, and exactly the kind of damp, cool air that urushi lacquer requires to cure correctly. Yamamoto had spent twelve years apprenticed to a lacquerware master in Ishikawa before setting up his own studio.

The studio has never advertised. New clients arrive by recommendation only. The waiting list for a URWA commission is currently four years. Yamamoto-sensei considers this an acceptable situation.

Our Principles

Three commitments that have shaped every piece

漆
Urushi Only

We use only authentic urushi — the sap of the Toxicodendron vernicifluum tree — not synthetic lacquer. True urushi is toxic until cured, difficult to source, slow to work, and irreplaceable in its depth and durability. It is the only material we accept.

層
Forty Layers Minimum

Each URWA piece receives a minimum of forty individual lacquer layers, each applied by brush, each cured in a controlled humidity chamber for 24–48 hours, each sanded before the next is applied. We have made pieces with over two hundred layers. You cannot see the layers. You can feel the depth.

命
Living Material

Urushi is a biological material. A properly maintained URWA piece will last five hundred years. An improperly stored one may degrade in fifty. We provide every client with a detailed care protocol, written by hand, and we offer lifetime restoration for any piece we have made.

Heritage

Four decades of patient work

1983
The Studio Opens

Kenji Yamamoto establishes URWA in a converted machiya townhouse in Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto, with three drying chambers and a single drying room.

1997
Living National Treasure

Yamamoto-sensei is designated a Living National Treasure (Ningen Kokuhō) by the Agency for Cultural Affairs for his mastery of urushi.

2008
Apprentice Programme

The first formal apprentice programme is established. Three positions are offered; candidates must commit to a minimum seven-year residency in Kyoto.

2021
Museum Commissions

URWA completes restoration commissions for the Tokyo National Museum, the Victoria & Albert, and the Musée Guimet — the studio's most prestigious institutional work.
